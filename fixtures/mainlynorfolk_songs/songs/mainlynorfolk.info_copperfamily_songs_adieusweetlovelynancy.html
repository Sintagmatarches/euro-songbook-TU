Adieu Sweet Lovely Nancy is one of the best-known songs from the repertoire of the Copper Family. It was first collected in 1898 from James and Thomas Copper by Kate Lee—the first ever song collected from them—and was published in first issue of the Journal of the Folk Song Society, Vol. 1, No. 1, in 1899. It is printed in The Copper Family Song Book and in Bob Copperâs book, A Song for Every Season. Bob and Jim Copper sang it on 1 March 1951 for the BBC recording 16060; this was included in 2001 on the Topic anthology of early Copper Family recordings, Come Write Me Down. Bob and John Copper sang it on their 4 LP box set of 1971, A Song for Every Season, that accompanied the book and Jill Copper and Jon Dudley sang it on the first Coppersongs LP.
