Sweep, Chimney Sweep

[ Roud 1217 ; Master title: Sweep, Chimney Sweep ; Ballad Index K240 ; Folkinfo 507 ; DT CHIMSWEP ; Mudcat 12131 ; trad.]

Sabine Baring-Gould: Songs of the West Bob Copper: Early to Rise Copper Family: The Copper Family Song Book.

Sweep, Chimney Sweep is a song from The Copper Family Song Book. Bob and Jim Copper sang it on a BBC recording made on 1 March 1951 by Brian George, which was included in 2001 on the Topic anthology of the Copper Family芒聙聶s early recordings, Come Write Me Down. Bob and Ron Copper sang it on the anthology Jack of All Trades (The Folk Songs of Britain Volume 3; Caedmon 1961; Topic 1968). Bob and John Copper sang the song in 1971 on the Copper Family芒聙聶s 4 LP box on the Leader Label, A Song for Every Season, and Bob, John, Jill and Lynne Copper and Jon Dudley sang it in 1988 on their EFDSS LP Coppersongs: A Living Tradition.

Steeleye Span (then with Martin Carthy and John Kirkpatrick) recorded Sweep, Chimney Sweep in 1977 for their tenth album, Storm Force Ten. A live recording from De Montfort Hall, Leicester on 11 February 1978 was released on the 2019 CD, possibly a bootleg, Live at De Monfort Hall. They also sang it live in 1995 at their 25th anniversary at The Forum, London, which was released on their 1999 CD The Journey.

White Hart sang Sweep, Chimney Sweep in 1979 on their Traditional Sound Recordings album In Search of Reward.

Regal Slip sang Sweep, Chim-nie Sweep in 1981 on their Dingle芒聙聶s album Bandstand. They noted:

Another Copper song, this time about that often unsung hero—the chimney sweep. Long considered fertility symbols, sweeps are still honoured guests at some weddings today, which accounts for the mild erotic innuendo contained in the song. The tune is possibly of French origin.

The Wilson Family sang Sweep, Chimney Sweep in 1991 on their The Wilson Family Album.

Jon Boden sang Sweep Chimney Sweep as the 27 November 2010 entry of his project A Folk Song a Day.

Matt Quinn sang Sweep Chimney Sweep, “a curious little song from the singing of Bob and Jim Copper”, on his 2017 CD The Brighton Line.
