The Bold Dragoon / Bold Keeper / Only a Soldier

[ Roud 321 ; Master title: The Bold Dragoon ; Laws M27 ; Ballad Index LM27 ; Bodleian Roud 321 ; GlosTrad Roud 321 ; Wiltshire 2 , 1042 ; DT DOUGTRD2 ; Mudcat 170818 ; trad.]

The Bold Dragoon is a song from the repertoire of the Copper Family. Bob Copper sang it in a BBC recording (BBC 21547) made by Peter Kennedy at the Central Club, Peacehaven, on 2 February 1955. He also collected The Bold Dragoon from Enos White of Axford, Hampshire, in July 1955, and published it in 1973 in his book Songs and Southern Breezes. He recorded it in 1977 on his Topic LP Sweet Rose in June. John Copper and Jon Dudley sang it in 1998 on the familyâs CD Coppersongs 3: The Legacy Continues.

Heather Wood sang The Bold Dragoon in 1967 on The Young Traditionâs second album, So Cheerfully Round. She commented in the album liner notes:

The Bold Dragoon was collected by Bob Copper from Enos White of Axford, Hampshire. It has connections with Earl Brand (Child 7) but this version is far nearer to popular taste than the morbid saga which the longer ballad relates, in which the Earl, after killing off his father and seven men (brothers in some versions) dies, and is followed rapidly to the grave by his beloved. Standard ballad ending about red roses and briars. Enos White started work on the farm when he was seven; he drove a two horse plough at the age of eight, For most of his life he was a carter. When this song was collected in 1955 he was working as a gardener.
