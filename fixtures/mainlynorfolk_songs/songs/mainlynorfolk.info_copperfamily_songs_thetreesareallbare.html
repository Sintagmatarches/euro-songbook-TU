Christmas Song / The Trees Are All Bare

[ Roud 1170 / Song Subject MAS718 ; Ballad Index CopSe202 ; Folkinfo 203 , 876 ; Mudcat 15944 ; trad.]

Copper Family: The Copper Family Song Book. Ken Stubbs: The Life of a Man

Christmas Song is a song from the repertoire of the Copper Family as printed in The Copper Family Song Book. Bob Copper sang it in 1971 on the Copper Familyâs Leader Records box set A Song for Every Season. John Copper and Jon Dudley sang it in 1995 on the album Coppersongs 2, and the Copper Family sang it in 2007 on Coppers at Christmas.

George Townshend of Lewes, Sussex, sang The Trees Theyâre All Bare in 1960-64 to Brian Matthews and in 1960 to Ken Stubbs. Three recordings were included in 2000 on his Musical Traditions anthology Come, Hand to Me the Glass. Ken Stubbs also printed it in 1970 in his book of English folk songs from the Home Counties, The Life of a Man. Brian Matthews and Rod Stradling commented in the the Musical Traditions albumâs booklet:

A song unknown outside Sussex, it would seem—Roud knows of only two singers apart from George who knew it. One was the Downs shepherd, Michael Blann from Upper Beeding, whose MS songbook is in the Sussex Library, and whose story is told in the Andrews book Shepherd of the Downs. The other singer(s) being various specified and unspecified members of the Copper family.

The song crops up in a four part harmony version in Sussex, suggesting that, like Dame Durden, it made an easy crossing between written and oral tradition.

Magpie Lane sang The Trees Are All Bare in 1995 on their Beautiful Jo album Wassail! A Country Christmas, and Andy Turner included two Magpie Lane versions from December 2013 as the 28 December 2014 entries of his project A Folk Song a Week. The CD booklet noted:

The winter landscape, wonderfully evoked in a song which is 19th-century or earlier in origin. This version is collected from the Copper family of Sussex.

Chris Molan and Harry Langston used to sing The Trees Are All Bare at the Golden Fleece in Stroud in the early 2000s, referring to the Copper family version. This recording was included in 2005 on the Musical Traditions anthology Songs From the Golden Fleece.

Bob Lewis sang The Trees Are All Bare on his 2003 CD The Painful Plough. Vic Smith noted:

To the Copper family this delightful song, with fewer verses, is Christmas Song. It was also in the school notebook where Michael Blann, the Upper Beeding shepherd, wrote his songs down. (Colin Andrews published these as Shepherd of the Downs in 1979). It appears in two 18th century printed song books and turns up a few times on London broadsides. Bob remembers two Georges, Townsend and Belton, singing this, but he first remembers it being sung at home.
