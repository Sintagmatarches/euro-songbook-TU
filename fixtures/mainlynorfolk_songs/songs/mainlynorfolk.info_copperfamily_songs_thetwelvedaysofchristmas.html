The Twelve Days of Christmas

[ Roud 68 / Song Subject MAS766 ; G/D 3:637 ; Ballad Index FO213 ; Bodleian Roud 68 ; Wiltshire 500 ; Folkinfo 761 ; DT XMAS12DY ; Mudcat 12518 ; trad.]

The Twelve Days of Christmas is a song from the repertoire of the Copper Family. Bob and Ron Copper sang it in a Peter Kennedy recording on Folk Song Today (1955) and on Songs of Ceremony (The Folk Songs of Britain Volume 9, Caedmon 1961; Topic 1970). In his song book of 1936 Jim Copper called this song Christmas Presents, and this title has been used for the reissue of Bob and Ron Copperâs 1950s recording on the 2007 CD Coppers at Christmas.

Isla St Clair, Martin Carthy and others sang The Twelve Days of Christmas in 1982 on the video 70 Golden Nursery Rhymes.

Sonya Cohen sang The Twelve Days of Christmas on the Seeger Familyâs 1989 Rounder album American Folk Songs for Christmas; most songs on it are from Ruth Crawford Seegerâs 1953 book on the same name.

John Kirkpatrick et al. sang The Twelve Days of Christmas on the Folkworks project and subsequent 1998 Fellside CD Wassail!. He noted:

The giving of gifts at Midwinter has been going on since ancient times. So has the singing of daft songs as memory tests and tongue twisters, of which this is a fine example. I canât help thinking that the words may originally have been a recipe for one of those feasts where each bird is stuffed inside a larger one, but I accept that this theory doesnât hold water once we get to nine lads a-leaping, even though it might explain their eagerness to keep on the move. This slightly unusual list of presents comes from the repertoire of the Copper family of Rottingdean, Sussex.

Bill Barclay sang an increasingly drunk The Twelve Days of Christmas on the 2002 Greentrax anthology Bah! Humbug.

The McCalmans sang The Twelve Folk Days of Christmas in 2006 on their Greentrax album Scots Abroad. This track was included in 2010 on their anthology The Greentrax Years.

Fiona Mackenzie sang Air a chiadh latha den Nollaig (Twelve Days of Christmas) in 2007 on her Gaelic Christmas album Duan Nollaig .

Nowell Sing We Clear sang The Twelve Days of Christmas in 2008 on their CD Nowell Nowell Nowell. They noted:

Josienne Clarke and Ben Walker sang The Twelve Days of Christmas in 2013 on their CD Midwinter.
