Sally Free and Easy

[ Roud - ; DT SALLFREE ; Mudcat 109027 ; Cyril Tawney]

Cyril Tawney wrote Sally Free and Easy, one of his best known songs, in 1958. He recorded it in 1970 for his 1972 Argo album In Port; this recording was included in 1997 on the anthology New Electric Muse II. He also sang it as title track of his 1989 Neptune cassette Sally Free and Easy which was reissued in 2003 with two bonus tracks as the CD Navy Cuts: The Songs of Cyril Tawney. Cyril Tawney wrote on his now defunct own website about his song:

Like any other admirer of the blues I had always assumed that its lyrics could only be imitated, not developed. Whether you were being serious or only writing a send-up, the words had to sound as if they were coming from an ordinary Southern American Negro.
