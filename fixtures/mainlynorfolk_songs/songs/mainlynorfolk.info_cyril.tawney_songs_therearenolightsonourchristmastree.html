There Are No Lights on Our Christmas Tree

[ Roud - ; DT NOLIGHTS ; Mudcat 64860 ; Cyril Tawney]

Cyril Tawney wrote There Are No Lights on Our Christmas Tree in 1962. He sang it in 1997 on his cassette of non-maritime songs, Man of Honour where he noted:

A lament from a child who is clearly more of a traditionalist than his father. When Dad is watching TV he wants complete silence and no superfluous illumination, and that goes for Christmas too. Reference to the series “Laramie” dates the song, but Iâve never been able to modernise it satisfactorily. The very rudimentary guitar work is intended for anybody who has been given one for Christmas and hasnât learned to play it yet.

This track was also included in 2002 on the brilliant 2002 anthology Bah! Humbug: The Alternative Christmas Album. Ian Green noted:

Cyril Tawney (England) does not regard himself as a singer-songwriter in the accepted sense, his main hobby being traditional song. However, although he seems to be best-known for his serious self-penned songs, such as The Grey Funnel Line, Sammyâs Bar, The Oggie Man and Sally Free and Easy, he is equally keen on putting together humorous pieces like the two items on this album.

In addition to the social comment on a father who did not want any illumination in the room when he was watching television, not even from the Christmas Tree, Cyril imagined the sort of improvised percussive accompaniment from saucepans and other household items providing backing for someone who had just received their first guitar as a present and only knew how to thwack the three basic cords.
