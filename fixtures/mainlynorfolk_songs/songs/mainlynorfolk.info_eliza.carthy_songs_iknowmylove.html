I Know My Love

[ Roud 60 ; Laws P25 ; Ballad Index FSWB143 , LP25 ; DT KNOWLOVE ; Mudcat 20116 ; trad.]

Susan Reid sang I Know My Love in 1954 on her Elektra album Susan Reid Sings Old Airs.

James McPeake of Belfast sang I Know My Love, accompanying himself on the harp, on the McPeake Familyâs 1963 Topic EP Wild Mountain Thyme. This EP was also included in 2009 on their same-named Topic reissue CD Wild Mountain Thyme.

The Ian Campbell Folk Group sang I Know My Love in 1964 on their Transatlantic album Across the Hills. They noted:

We learnt this from the singing of James McPeake, a member of the famous McPeake family of Belfast. He sings it in a much more leisurely fashion than this, with a harp accompaniment, but Irish friends have told us that in the West coast of IreIand it is sung in a light staccato fashion, and in much stricter time. Close investigation of the tempo will disclose that it is actually in 3/4 time, but in order to prevent it degenerating into an old-fashioned waltz, we have superimposed a 4/4 tempo on the banjo.

Davy Graham played I Know My Love in 1969 on his Decca album The Holly Kaleidoscope.

Fraser Bruce sang I Know My Love on his 1974 album Fareweel tae Tarwathie. Drew Moyes noted:

The last of the three Irish tracks on this record. Although this version is undoubtedly Irish, related songs exist in many places around the world.

A different mix (with a longer intro and a repeated chorus at the end) was later included in their compilation album On Reflection. Eliza Carthy noted on the original album:

Poachers Pocket sang I Know My Love in 1994 on their WildGoose album Fair Game. They noted:

This pretty song is about a girl who is worried she might lose her man. Maybe someone should tell her that being good at housework is not necessarily the best approach to the problem.

German duo Sperris & Wicca sang I Know My Love in 1999 on their eponymous album Sperris & Wicca.

Anna Tam sang I Know My Love on her 2021 CD Anchoress. She noted:

An Irish traditional song collected by Herbert Hughes and first published in his Irish Country Songs by Boosey and Hawkes in 1909.
