Next Monday Morning is a song from the repertoire of Norfolk singer Harry Cox. Peter Kennedy recorded him singing this song at home in Catfield, Norfolk, in October 1953. This recording was included in 1965 on his EFDSS LP Traditional English Love Songs.

Frank Purslow and John Pearse sang Monday Morning on their 1960 album Rap-a-Tap-Tap: English Folk Songs Miss Pringle Never Taught Us, and Purslow included this as Married Next Monday Morning in his book The Foggy Dew.

William Harrison Burnett of Fayetteville, AR, sang Iâll Get Married A-Sunday in a recording made by Sandy Paton in 1962. It was included in 2000 on the Folk-Legacy anthology Ballads and Songs of Tradition.

John Goodluck sang Monday Morning in 1977 on his Traditional Sound album Mondayâs Childe. He noted:

The mere thought of Monday morning raises a shudder from many people but my version of this old Harry Cox song is totally different, with another young bride-to-be eagerly awaiting that very moment. Any kind of jubilation seems to attract one dismal Jimmy to dampen the atmosphere; “for young men are false” says the old man, warning the fifteen year old girl, but she is not to be deterred so easily and we are left with the hope and promise of a happy ending.
