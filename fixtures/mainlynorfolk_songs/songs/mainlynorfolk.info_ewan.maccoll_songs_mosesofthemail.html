Moses of the Mail

[ Roud - ; Mudcat 128515 ; Ewan MacColl]

Karl Dallas: One Hundred Songs of Toil Ewan MacColl: The Shuttle and Cage

Ewan MacColl recorded his own song Moses of the Mail in 1951 for a 78rpm record (Topic TRC51). He published this song in 1954 in his book The Shuttle and Gage and recorded it for on the same-named 10" LP in 1957, Shuttle and Gage (which later was part of his 12" LP Steam Whistle Ballads). “Moses” was the nickname of Henry Poyser, an engine-driver who served on the Manchester-Warrington run in the 80âs. Despite the “local” feeling of the text and the trivial nature of the events described, the song still lives as part of the oral tradition of the Lancashire railwaymen. The version sung here is collated from three texts collected in Newton-Heath Loco shed in 1952.

Tony Rose sang Moses of the Mail in 1977 on the Broadside album Steam Ballads.

Mick Groves sang Moses of the Mail on his 2004 album of songs of Ewan MacColl, Fellow Journeyman.
