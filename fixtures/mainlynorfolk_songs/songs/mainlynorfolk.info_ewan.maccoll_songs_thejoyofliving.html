The Joy of Living

[ Roud - ; DT JOYLIVE ; Mudcat 42500 ; new words and trad. arr. Ewan MacColl, tune trad. Sicilian]

Ewan MacColl wrote The Joy of Living in 1986. He and Peggy Seeger sang it in the same year on their Blackthorne album Items of News. He alsio sang it in 1990 on his Cooking Vinyl album Black and White. This track was also included in 1993 on the Cooking Vinyl Sampler Vol. 1. Alison McMorland and Geordie McIntyre sang Joy of Living in 2001 on their Tradition Bearers album Rowan in the Rock. They noted:

The contribution of Ewan MacColl (1915-89) to the folk song revival has been immense, excelling as a poet, playwright, actor, singer and songwriter. In a lifetime of song-smithing such classics as The Manchester Rambler and Shoals of Herring spring immediately to mind.

At the age of 72 he was climbing Suilven in Sutherland and failed to reach the summit. He later wrote: “My mountain days were over. I sat down on a rock feeling desolate. This lasted several days. My grief and sense of loss gave way to nostalgia and I wrote The Joy of Living. In an odd way it helped me come to terms with my old age.” (and the limits imposed by his ill-health). Ewan described the song as “his testament”, many regard it as his finest…

We learned it from the fine singing of our friend Gus Langlands.
