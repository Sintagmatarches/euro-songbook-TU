This song has a sexual theme. Purslow states: “This song was printed by the Seven Dials song-sheet merchants about 1840, and is probably much older. The refrain “so green as the leaves” is not just word spinning; this imagery is quite common in British Folk Song, and this symbolic phrase would have received immediate recognition from the Elizabethans. It was Polonius who said of his daughter, Ophelia, “She Is a green girl” (Hamlet). The adjective is still in common use, of course, but has lost much of its original significance.”
