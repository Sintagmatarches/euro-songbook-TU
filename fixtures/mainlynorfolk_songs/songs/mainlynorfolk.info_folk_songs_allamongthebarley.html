All Among the Barley / The Ripe and Bearded Barley

[ Roud 1283 ; Ballad Index BdAAtBar ; Bodleian Roud 1283 ; Wiltshire 670 ; DT AMNGBARL ; Mudcat 43713 , 105599 ; Elizabeth Stirling]

Alfred Williams: Folk-Songs of the Upper Thames

Alfred Williams collected The Ripe and Bearded Barley from farm hand Henry Sirman of Stanton Harcourt, and printed it in his 1923 book Folk-Songs of the Upper Thames.

Walter Pardon of Knapton, Norfolk, sang this song with the more common title All Among the Barley on 2 August 1978 to Mike Yates. This recording was included in 2000 on his Musical Traditions anthology Put a Bit of Powder on It, Father. Rod Stradling and Mike Yates noted in the albumâs booklet:

This is the only instance, according to Roud, of this song being recorded in the tradition, and he only knows of it being collected once elsewhere—by Alfred Williams from Henry [Sirman] of Stanton Harcourt in Oxfordshire … although Henry Burstow included the title in his repertoire list. The remainder of his 14 instances are all from broadsides or books.

Johnny Collins sang All Among the Barley on his 1982 Traditional Sound album Free & Easy; it was also included in 1998 on his anthology The Best of the Early Years. He noted:

This is from Alfred Williams, Folk-Songs of the Upper Thames, [and] is set to a superbly singable tune by Mike Gabriel of Cheltenham. I got it from Maureen ‘Mo’ Marshall.

Heritage sang Ripe and Bearded Barley in 1982 on their Plant Life album Living by the Air. They noted:

As a beer drinking nation the English have penned hundreds of songs in praise of the barley crop. This one was collected from the Upper Thames area and has a fine chorus.

Ron Taylor and Jeff Gillett sang All Amongst the Barley in 2006 on their WildGoose CD Both Shine as One. They noted:

A glorious evocation of harvest time, and one of several songs on this album documenting a love/hate relationship with this delightful and nutritious cereal. Tune by Mike Gabriel, who found the words in a book of poems but canât now remember the book or the author. Fourth verse added by Ron.

Piers Cawley sang All Around the Barley on his 2020 download album Isolation Sessions #2. He noted:

I first heard this sung by Anne Briggs at the Bay Folk Club in Cullercoats int the early nineties. It was the last song of her set and it was revelatory. Got me right between the eyes, so it did.
