Jacqui McShee and the John Renbourn Group sang A Maid in Bedlam as the title track of their 1977 album, A Maid in Bedlam. The album notes commented:

A Maid in Bedlam is a reworking of an early song in which a black, George Sighous, was in Bedlam for his mad love for an English girl. Taken from a single song sheet with music, c. 1740, called The Blackâs Lamentation.

Geoff Jerram sang Bedlam (The Loyal Lover) in 2006 as one of the title tracks of his Forest Tracks album Bedlam. He noted:

This is one of the ‘Lovers Reunited’ group of love songs in which boy is dispatched to the wars to keep him away from daughter. She mourns his loss until he returns to reclaim his true love. There is no deception and no token, simply a happy ending. The story is similar to Bedlam (track 6). The printed version of the refrain ends with “I love my love because I know he first loved me”. The version I prefer and sing here I heard a long time ago and I think it makes more sense.

Jon Boden sang Maid in Bedlam as the 20 April 2011 entry of his project A Folk Song a Day.

Sarah Matthews sang Abroad As I Was Walking as the title track of her 2012 album, As I Was Walking. She commented in her online notes:

I learnt this song during my work with Tim Arthur as musical director for Bold Nelsonâs Praise in 2005—thanks Tim and Dave, as I had not come across it before. The sad tale of so many women locked away in Asylums in the good old days.

Keith Kendrick and Sylvia Needham sang Abroad As I Was Walking on their 2018 WildGoose CD Shine On. He noted:

This wonderful version of Bedlam from Book 2 of The Crystal Spring has been part of my personal repertoire since Barry Coope and I sang it in duo around the late 70s.
