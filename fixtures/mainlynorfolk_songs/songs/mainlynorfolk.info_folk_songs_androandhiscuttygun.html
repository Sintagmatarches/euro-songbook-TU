Andro and His Cutty Gun / Blythe, Blythe and Merry Was She

[ Roud 2868 ; Ballad Index MPCAACG ; DT CUTTYGUN ; trad.]

Ewan MacColl: Scotland Sings Folk Songs and Ballads of Scotland

Ewan MacColl sang Andro and His Cutty Gun in 1956 on his Riverside album Scots Drinking Songs. and in 1962 on his Dionysos album Merry Muses of Caledonia. He noted in the first album:

Burns described this as “one of the bonniest and certainly one of the most vigorous of our old songs”. As a record of a drinking party it is certainly unequaled in Scots national music.

The Clutha sang Andro and His Cutty Gun in 1974 on their Topic album of Scots ballads, songs and dance tunes, Scots Ballads, Songs & Dance Tunes. Don Martin noted:

One of the fine old songs of obscure origin which Robert Burns found so stimulating. A ‘cutty gun’ was a short tobacco pipe: a convenient phallic symbol. Andrew Cronshaw played the tune of Andro and His Cutty Gun on his 1982 album The Great Dark Water.

Andro (and His Cutty Gun) features regularly in the Scots song tradition. In this 17th Century episode he confirms his superhero status by standing everyone a round.

Jeana Leslie and Siobhan Miller sang Blythe, Blythe in 2010 on their Greentrax album Shadows Tall. They noted:
