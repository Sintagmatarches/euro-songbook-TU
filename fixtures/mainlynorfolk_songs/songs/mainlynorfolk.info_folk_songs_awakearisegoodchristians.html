Awake, Arise Good Christians

[ Roud 17709 ; trad.]

Awake, Arise Good Christians is a carol from the Sheffield village carols tradition. Carollers at the Royal Hotel in Dungworth sang Awake, Arise Good Christians on 2 December 1973 on the Leader LP of a singing tradition recorded in South Yorkshire pubs, A Peopleâs Carol. Another recording from the Royal Hotel in 1995 was included on the CD of traditional Christmas Carolling from the Southern Pennines, English Village Carols. Prof. Ian Russell commented in the albumâs booklet:

This well-loved local carol is a favourite in Stannington, Dungworth, Worrall, Oughtibridge, and Ecclesfield. Its lyrics serve as a remainder that several of these carols were written to be sung out of doors in the middle of a winterâs night when most people would be tucked up in bed asleep. Oral tradition attributes it to William Mount of Worrall, before 1880, who was the leader of the Big Set and played the cello. As an aid to memory, the carollers delight in assigning fanciful lyrics to the tunes of different symphonies, this one being “When the beer is on the table…” The carol has not been found outside the district.

Ian Russellâs Village Carols website lists a longer version (Awake, Arise Good Christians) than the one sung in Sheffield and shown here. The American Library of Congress credits it to W.F. Sherwin (New York: Biglow & Main, 1871). This version has five verses, two that are also in the Sheffield version and three others.
