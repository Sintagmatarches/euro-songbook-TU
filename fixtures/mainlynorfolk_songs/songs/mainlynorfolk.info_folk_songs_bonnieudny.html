More than one song praises the green beauty of Udny, 12 miles north east of Aberdeen, but Gavin Greig pointed out that versions of this favorite song can be found in praise of locations not just elsewhere in Scotland, but also in Ireland and England.

Stanley Robertson sang Bonnie Udny on his 2009 Elphinstone institute anthology The College Boy. Thomas A. McKean and Sara Reith noted:

This song holds a special place in the hearts of Travellers, partly due to its lyric content and partly because Udny green was long a favourite camping site for Travelling families, one where they felt welcomed by the community as they made their seasonal rounds. It was popular in the settled population as well; there are more than twenty versions in the Greig-Duncan collection. The song has found its way to the Borders of Scotland where it was collected from Willie Scott, variants are found elsewhere in the British Isles, and the theme of praise of place generally a popular one. “Ye shine four ye stan” in particular, is found as a commonplace in a number of songs, such as Glenlogie (Roud 101) and Bonnie Portmore/Portrush (Roud 3475).
