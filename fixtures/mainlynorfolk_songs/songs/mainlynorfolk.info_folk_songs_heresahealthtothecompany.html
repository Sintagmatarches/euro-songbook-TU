Its strong tune has also been used for other Irish traditional songs and for the American anthem, The Liberty Song. The song might be of Ulster origin, perhaps derived from a Scottish original. Robin Morton lists it in Folksongs Sung in Ulster, and Paddy Tunney learned the song from North Antrim singer Joe Holmes. I first heard Joe and Len sing it together at a singing festival in Trinity College, Dublin. No other traditional singer did more to build bridges in a divided community and replace hatred and discord with harmony and love.
