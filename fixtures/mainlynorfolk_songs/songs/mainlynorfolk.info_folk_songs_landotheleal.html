Written in 1798 on the death of the baby daughter of her friend Mrs. Campbell Colquhoun (born Mary Ann Erskine). Authorship was not acknowledged until Lady Nairne was approaching old age, when she wrote:

The land of the leal is a happy rest for the mind in this dark pilgrimage… O yes, I was young then. I wrote it merely because I liked the air so much, and I put these words to it, never fearing questions as to the authorship… I was present when it was asserted that Burns composed it on his deathbed, and that he had ‘Jean’ instead of ‘John’, but the parties could not decide why it never appeared in his works, as his last song should have done.
