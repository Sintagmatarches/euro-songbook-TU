Let the Sun Shine Down on Me

[ Roud - ; Mudcat 85261 ; Than Hall (Jean Ritchie)]

Jean Ritchie sang her own song Let the Sun Shine Down on Me in 1965 on her Warner album A Time for Singing. She noted:

This is, I guess, a protest against protest songs … most of them are entirely grim and without hope. The tune here is from an old mountain hymn, the words came with the “ban-the-bomb” theme in mind, but the whole song is full of hope and praise and joy, as all of life should be.

Jenn Butterworth and Laura-Beth Salter sang Let the Sun Shine Down on Me on their 2016 album Bound. This video shows them in a home concert in March 2020:
