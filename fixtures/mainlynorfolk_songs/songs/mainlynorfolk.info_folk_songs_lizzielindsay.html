Hermes Nye sang Lizzie Lindsay in 1957 on his Folkways album of early English ballads from the Percy and Child collections, Ballad Reliques.

Belle Stewart sang Leezie Lindsay on her 1977 Topic album Queen Among the Heather which was re-released in 1998 as a Greentrax CD. Geordie McIntyre noted:

This ballad has appeared in fragmented form in just about every ‘standard’ collection of Scots song and been equally subject to stilted and standard drawing-room treatment. Belleâs lively version is musically very akin to the air collected by Robert Burns for the Scots Musical Museum, 1796. Indeed, the opening verse, used commonly as a chorus in ‘standard’ literary versions, was collected by Burns and sent to the Museum along with the tune. The rest of the verses are close to some appearing in Childâs ‘B’ text from the Kinloch MSS.

Variants upon the basic Lizzie Lindsay story, which in turn has similarities with Glasgow Peggy (Child 228), are to be found in such lengthy narratives as The Blaeberry Courtship [Roud 1888] and Orange and Blue [Roud 567]. A version of the former appears in Ord, Bothy Songs and Ballads, and of the latter in Greig, Folk-Song of the North-East. Good oral versions are extant.

Tony and Jenny Reavill sang Leezie Lindsay in 1980 on their Hill & Dale album Revelation.

Arthur Johnstone sang one verse of Leezie Lindsay in 1996 on the Linn Anthology The Complete Songs of Robert Burns Volume 2.

Eddi Reader sang Leezie Lindsay on her 2009 album The Songs of Robert Burns - Deluxe Edition.

Rosaleen Gregory sang Lizzie Lindsay in 2013 as the last track of her second album of Child ballads, Serpentâs Knee. She tersely noted:
