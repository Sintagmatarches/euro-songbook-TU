Ewan MacColl sang the transportation ballad My Last Farewell to Stirling in 1956 on his Riverside album Scots Street Songs. He noted:

This is one of the few exclusively Scots broadsheet ballads. It was sung originally in the tempo of a slow strathspey, but it is usually sung in waltz time today. It is a transportation ballad of a high order, though it never attained the universal popularity of Van Diemenâs Land. I know it from the singing of Willie Mathieson of Castleton and Jamie Taylor of Fyvie.
