Now Our Meeting Is Over / Land on the Shore

[ Roud 5716 ; Ballad Index WA084 ; Mudcat 22953 , 84146 ; trad.]

Sandy and Caroline Paton sang Now, My Friends, the Meeting Is Over in 1966 on their Folk-Legacy album Folksongs and Ballads. They noted:

This song, as it is sung by Mrs. Buna Hicks, closes Volume I of The Traditional Music of Beech Mountain, North Carolina (Folk-Legacy FSA-22). Mrs. Hicks said that the song was regularly used to end the religious services, or “camp meetings”, in her area, years ago, and that her mother was always asked to sing it. For other versions of the hymn, see Lomax (American Ballads and Folk Songs, p. 571) and Jackson (Another Sheaf of White Spirituals, p. 10).

We worked out the harmony for this Dorian tune quite by accident. Although the intervals seemed strange at first, after awhile harmonizing within the mode became quite natural.

The Fagans sang Stand by the Shore on their 2002 CD Turning Fine. They noted:

On a rowdy bus trip after one Victor Harbour Festival, Scott and Louisa Wise from Margaret River in Western Australia taught us this fabulous hymn. We were thrilled to sing it with the whole Wise family at the Canberra National Festival in 2002 and again in the spectacular acoustics of Western Australiaâs Fairbridge chapel.

Cath and Phil Tyler sang Fathers Now Our Meeting Is Over in 2015 on their EP The Song-Crowned King.

Lady Maisery sang Land on the Shore in 2016 on their RootBeat CD Cycle. They noted:

An American farewell song that may have originally been a Shaker hymn. The tune for our version was collected by Sam P. Bayard of Pennsylvania and was included in John and Alan Lomaxâs American Ballads and Folk Songs (1934). In the past year, this song has taken on a new relevance for us in light of the huge numbers of displaced people in the world hoping to find a new life on a safer shore.
