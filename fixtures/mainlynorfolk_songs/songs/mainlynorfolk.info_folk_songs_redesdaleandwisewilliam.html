Redesdale and Wise William

[ Roud 243 / Song Subject MAS1558 ; Child 246 ; Ballad Index C246 ; DT REDESDL1 ; Mudcat 83619 ; trad.]

Emily Lyle, Kaye McAlpine, Anne Dhu McLucas: The Song Repertoire of Amelia and Jane Harris

Chris Coe sang Redesdale and Wise William in 2001 on her Backshift album A Wiser Fool. She noted:

Conjectural restoration again—Child and Bronson. Two lager-louts out on the town boasting about their prowess with women and making drunken bets. No change there then! Tap-dancing carrier pigeons as a bonus!

Chris Coe also sang this song at the Fife Traditional Singing Festival, Collessie, Fife in May 2008, which was included in the following year on the festival anthology Grand to Be a Working Man (Old Songs & Bothy Ballads Volume 5). Peter Shepheard noted:

Text and tune adapted by Chris Coe from the Perthshire repertoire of the Harris sisters written down in the early 1800s. Amelia Harris learned the ballad in childhood from her old nurse and no other tune appears to have been found, The ballad is included in F.J. Child芒聙聶s English and Scottish Popular Ballads (Child 246) where he has three versions. The tune and text from the Harris sisters are included in Bertrand Bronson芒聙聶s The Traditional Tunes of the Child Ballads Volume 4 (1972). The songs and ballads of the Harris sisters are the subject of a recent monograph by Emily Lyle et al: The Song Repertoire of Amelia and Jane Harris, Scottish Text Society (2002).
