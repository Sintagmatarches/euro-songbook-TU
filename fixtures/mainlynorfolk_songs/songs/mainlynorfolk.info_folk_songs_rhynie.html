The song as sung by John Strachan was included in Norman Buchan芒聙聶s influential 101 Scottish Songs published in 1962 and is in the Greig-Duncan Folk-Song Collection (GD 348) under the title Jock o Rhynie. An old man, William Forsyth, told Greig that he “remembered as a boy his mother sing Jock o Rhynie and this would take the song back to say 1830”. The farmer at Mains o Rhynie (in the high glens of Auchindoir in Strathbogie about 8 miles south of Huntly) from 1830 until his death in 1851 was John Gordon and he was known as ‘Jock o Rhynie’—but there may have been earlier Jocks at Rhynie and the song could well be earlier.
