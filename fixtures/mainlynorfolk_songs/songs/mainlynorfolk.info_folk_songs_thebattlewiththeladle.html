The Battle With the Ladle / A Rich Old Miser

[ Roud 1004 ; Laws Q7 ; Ballad Index LQ07 ; DT LADLESNG ; trad.]

Peggy Seeger sang A Rich Old Man on her 1958 Riverside album Folksongs and Ballads. She noted:

I learned this delightful song from Ballads and Songs of Southern Michigan by E.E. Gardner and G.J. Chickering. Songs of this type were quite widespread in Scotland, where poverty dictated the necessity of marrying young farm girls to wealthy old men. As often as not, this resulted in the cuckolding of the groom, a considerably slyer action than just hitting the old man on the bead.

She also sang it on her 1962 Topic EP Troubled Love. Angela Carter noted:

A caustic version of the potentially tragic situation of a young girl and an old husband. In this song the young wife is triumphant. It appears in Ballads and Songs of Southern Michigan by Gardner and Chickering.
