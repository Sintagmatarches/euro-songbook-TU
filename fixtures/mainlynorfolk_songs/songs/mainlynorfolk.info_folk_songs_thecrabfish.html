Under various titles, such as The Sea Crab or The Lobster, sung in Britain and America, and, although fairly common in later collections, it is probably only its bawdy nature which prevented its appearing more often in the papers of our Victorian and Edwardian predecessors. In Britain, the earliest known version is found in Bishop Percyâs Folio Manuscript, compiled c.1620-1650.
