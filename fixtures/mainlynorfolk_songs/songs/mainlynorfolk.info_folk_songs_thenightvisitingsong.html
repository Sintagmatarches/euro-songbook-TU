Night-visiting songs sometimes relate to “bundling”—a custom found in many cultures—essentially a consummation of love sanctioned by the girlâs parents on the night before the wedding. The theme is a common one and a similar text is found in the supernatural ballad, The Grey Cock. The girl is visited by the spirit of her sweetheart and the cock is begged not to crow, as this will signal the dawn and the loverâs ghost would have to leave. Many night-visiting songs have abandoned the metaphysical ingredient—so that, instead of calling the ghost back to the grave, the cock simply proclaims the start of another day for the working person.
