The tune is one of those characteristically Irish melodies which includes a changed leading note or fah, whichever way you want to look at it. The changing of the leading note by flattening, or of the fah by sharpening has the same effect as a modulating to the subdominant in the first case, or to the dominant in the second. You will hear this effect in the next two songs as well. The tune is in the Doh mode, though from what I have already said, you could well argue that it is in the Soh mode. However, if you try playing it in the Soh mode you will find that the harmonies grate. Lonan sang Blackbird on their 2021 album Forever Has Flown. They noted:

Robin [Timmis] found the words for this one in a book of traditional Scottish songs and set them to his own tune. Also known as The Royal Blackbird, we have always thought of this one as a Jacobite song. However, a little more digging uncovered that it appeared in broadside ballads from as early as 1651—so it would have first referred to King Charles II in exile following the execution of his father in 1649, before the allegorical ‘blackbird’ title later came to be associatedwith Bonnie Prince Charlie.
