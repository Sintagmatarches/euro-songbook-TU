John Goodluck sang The Smugglerâs Boy on his 1976 Sweet Folk and Country album Speed the Plough. He noted:

I got this set of words from the book I Walked by Night by Lilias Rider Haggard. The tune I got from Bob Hart and it seems to be the standard Smugglers Boy one. Concerns a young fellow stricken by bad luck early on, fights to overcome this and ends up being a lucky man (well rich anyway). He appeared to be a nameless hero so we called him Jimmy Allen and played a tune for him at the end.

Jackie Oates sang My Ship Lost Its Rigging in 2008 on her second album, The Violet Hour. She noted:

This song comes from a recording of Gloucester singer Biggun Smith, from January 1967, although I first heard it sung by Sam Lee. I was drawn to the song, as it seems to follow on from a favourite of mine, The Smugglers Boy to tell of the fate of the boy who is left orphaned on the sea shore.
