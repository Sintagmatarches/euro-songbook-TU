James Reeves included this version in 1958 in his book of English traditional verses from the mss of Cecil Sharp, The Idiom of the People. It was also included in 2007 in The Folk Handbook. The threshing machine would have been topical during the early years of the nineteenth century. According to the Encyclopaedia Britannica (14th edition), “A workable threshing machine was invented late in the eighteenth century and was gradually coming into use early in the nineteenth; it was driven by water or wind power, sometimes by horse labour, and later by steam. The Thrashing Machine is a well known, if not often reported, song.
