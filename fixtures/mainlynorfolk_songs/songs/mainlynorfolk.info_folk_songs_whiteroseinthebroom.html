The White Rose in the Broom / Burd Margaret

[ Roud 3184 ; trad. or John Brune]

The White Rose in the Broom is “a song from Abram Cooper of West Kent; collected by John Brune, August 1962” and printed in English Dance & Song 35:2 (1973) p.59, It is also listed in the Kent Trad collection in which Andy Turner is suspicious about the songâs origin:

It seems unlikely that these words were collected from a travelling singer—they look suspiciously like the work of a modern songwriter attempting to write a romantic ballad. John Brune certainly collected songs from travellers, but he also wrote songs, and seems to have had a reputation for ‘improving’ some of the songs he collected. There is no other entry in the Roud Index for number 3184, which tends to confirm these suspicions.

Nick Dow sang Burd Margaret in 1978 as the title track of his Dingleâs album Burd Margaret. He noted:

A strange song of murder and the supernatural. ‘Burd’ is an Anglo Saxon word meaning ‘fair’ or ‘good looking’. The original title of the song was White Rose in the Broom. The accompaniment is by Bonnie Shaljean on harmonium and Ruth Morgan on treble recorder.

Holly Clarke sang Burd Margaret on her 2026 album Wild, Feral, Fierce. She noted:

From the repertoire of the wonderful Nick Dow, Burd Margaret is a psychedelic fantasy landscape full of metaphor. Vivid images of a white rose growing from a womanâs mouth and knights being hung from a sycamore tree really drew me in as it felt like something out of a fantasy novel. Nick tells me this song is actually called The White Rose in the Broom and was published in the English folk dance and song magazine in 1978.
