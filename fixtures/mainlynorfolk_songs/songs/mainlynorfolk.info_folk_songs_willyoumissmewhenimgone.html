Will You Miss Me When I芒聙聶m Gone?

[ Roud 4440 ; Mudcat 18761 ; Rev. George Beebe, H.E. McAfee]

Will You Miss Me? is a song by Rev. George Beebe and H.E. McAfee printed in John B. Vaughan: Songs for the Singing, Normal and Literary Schools (Athens, GA, 1910, no.75, p.89). Rev. George R. Beebe was born on 9 June 1828, in Lancashire, England and died on 10 March 1877 in Grafton, New Hampshire, United States. (On Mudcat the author is given as Rev. George Beebee; there was a Rev. George Beebee 1851-19.05.1913, buried in Norwich, but this song seems to be an American one.)

The Carter Family recorded Will You Miss Me When I芒聙聶m Gone? on 10 May 1928 in Camden, New York for their 10" shellac record Victor 21638.

The Pennywhistlers sang Will You Miss Me When I芒聙聶m Gone? in 1966 as one of the two English songs of their Bounty/Nonesuch album Folksongs of Eastern Europe.

Royston and Heather Wood with Peter Bellamy sang Will You Miss Me?, “an American traditional song”, in 1977 on their Transatlantic duo album No Relation.

Sara Grey and Ellie Ellis got Will You Miss Me? from the Carter Family and sang it with Paul Downes, Keith Kendrick and Tony Gibbons in 1986 on their Greenwich Village album You Gave Me a Song….

Rod Clements, Ray Jackson and Rab Noakes sang Will You Miss Me? in 1986 on the Woodie Guthrie tribute album on Black Crow Records, Woody Lives!.

Elizabeth LaPrelle sang Will You Miss Me When I芒聙聶m Gone? on her 2011 album Birds芒聙聶 Advice. She noted:

More Carter Family here. Not to ruin the finer sentiments of the song for you, but the flowery lyrics make for a diverting farcical pantomime.
