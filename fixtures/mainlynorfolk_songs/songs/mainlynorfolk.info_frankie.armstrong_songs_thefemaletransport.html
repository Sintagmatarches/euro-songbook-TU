Frankie Armstrong sang The Female Transport in 1973 on her album Out of Love, Hope and Suffering. She noted:

After the loss of the American colonies, the British Government was at a loss to know what to do with its convicts. And so penal settlements were created in Australia. The most trivial offenses were enough to ensure transportation there for seven years, fourteen years, or for life. Many songs were written around this theme, most of them dealing with men.
