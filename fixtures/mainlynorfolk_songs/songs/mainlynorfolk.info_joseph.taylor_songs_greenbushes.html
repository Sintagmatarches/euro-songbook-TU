Mike Yates comments that this song (along with Lovely Joan, The Knight and the Shepherdâs Daughter, Seventeen Come Sunday, etc) is similar to the old French Pastourelles that date from the Middle Ages. In the Pastourelle a knight meets a shepherdess; the setting is usually Spring time (especially May). He tries to court her and offers her all sorts of presents (see Green Bushes) but she says that she is in love with a local shepherd. Usually that is the end of the affair. She goes off with the shepherd and the knight rides off alone. The themes are certainly mediaeval and some scholars have noted the similarity between them and the Song of Solomon.
