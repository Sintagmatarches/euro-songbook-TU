The Stanley Brothers recorded Little Glass of Wine on 3 March 1949 in Nashville, Tennessee. This recording was included in 2018 on the Musical Traditions anthology of Anglo-American songs and tunes from Texas to Maine, A Distant Land to Roam. Rod Stradling noted:

Although well-known as a traditional song in England and Scotland—where it goes under such titles as Jealousy, Oxford City, Down the Green Groves or Young Maria—it seems to be rather uncommon in rural America today. According to Steve Gardham (private correspondence) it can only be traced back to an eleven stanza version, titled Oxford City, which was originally printed by two London broadside printers, Pitts and Catnach.
