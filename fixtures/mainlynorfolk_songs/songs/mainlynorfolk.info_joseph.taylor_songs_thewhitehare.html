The White Hare / Howden Town

[ Roud 1110 / Song Subject MAS1486 ; Master title: The White Hare ; TYG 79 ; Ballad Index RcTWhiHa ; VWML FK/11/196/3 , PG/5/28 , PG/6/9 ; Bodleian Roud 1110 ; Folkinfo 425 ; Mudcat 25134 , 31010 ; trad.]

Paul & Liz Davenport: Down Yorkshire Lanes Frank Kidson: Traditional Tunes

A song about the hunt of an albino hare.

Joseph Taylor sang this as The White Hare of Oldham, which is probably a mis-hearing of Howden. It was recorded by Percy Grainger in 1908, released in 1972 on the LP Unto Brigg Fair, and included in 1998 on the Topic anthology To Catch a Fine Buck Was My Delight (The Voice of the People Volume 18). The original album芒聙聶s sleeve notes state:

Other versions KTT, KP, SM, PB. Sound recordings BBC 19336. Tom and Jim Smith are names that occur with great frequency through successive generations of huntsmen in the employ of the Earl of Yarborough especially in the eighteenth and early nineteenth centuries.

The Watersons with Mike in lead sang The White Hare of Howden with their own chorus on their eponymous 1966 album, The Watersons. Like all but one tracks from this LP, it was re-issued in 1994 on their CD Early Days. A.L. Lloyd noted:

Frank Kidson obtained this from his indefatigable assistant, Mr. Lolley, and printed it in his Traditional Tunes. This version, the one the Watersons sing, locates the hunt firmly in Yorkshire but the song has been collected elsewhere. Percy Grainger, a pioneer in the use of mechanical recording devices with which to collect folk songs, recorded the fine singer Joseph Taylor singing it in 1906. Taylor came from Brigg, Lincolnshire, far across the Humber from the Watersons home at Hull. They made up their own words for the chorus.

Martin Carthy also recorded White Hare (of Howden) with Dave Swarbrick in 1968 for their album But Two Came By; it was also included in 1971 on the compilations This Is… Martin Carthy and Focus on Folk. His chorus is very similar to that of Joseph Tailor. Shirley Collins, Royston Wood and Lal and Mike Waterson sang a nearly identical version of The White Hare in 1971 on No Roses.

Vulcan芒聙聶s Hammer sang The White Hare of Howden on their 1973 album True Hearts and Sound Bottoms.

Brian Dewhurst with Tom Tiddler芒聙聶s Ground sang The White Hare of Howden in 1975 on their Folk Heritage album The Hunter and the Hunted. He noted:

From the singing of Joseph Taylor of Saxby-All-Saints collected by Percy Grainger in 1906. The chorus is taken from a Yorkshire version of this song.

The High Level Ranters sang The White Hare of Howden in 1977 on their and Martyn Wyndham-Read芒聙聶s Broadside album English Sporting Ballads.

Brian Peters sang The Oldham White Hare in 1989 on his Harbourtown album Fools of Fortune. He noted:

While animals in folklore usually have special qualities, and The Oldham White Hare is surely one for the roll of honour that includes sundry white horses, hinds and whales, I sing this song in tribute to my friend and mentor, the late Harry Boardman, who had it from Trevor Smallet of Saddleworth.

Graham Metcalfe sang Howden Town in 1996 on his WildGoose CD Songs From Yorkshire and Other Civilisations. He noted:

Politics aside, the hunting world has given us some great songs. This version is from the singing of Joseph Taylor, Lincolnshire.

John Roberts & Tony Barrand sang The White Hare in 1998 on their CD of English folksongs collected by Percy Grainger, Heartoutbursts. They noted:

A hunting song from Joseph Taylor. This was on one of the seven discs he made for the Gramophone Company. Grainger had recorded one stanza in 1906. It is interesting to note that Mr Taylor芒聙聶s memory of texts was not his strong point, and in many instances he could recall few, if any, verses to a song. Fortunately this failing did not seem to extend to his memory for tunes, which, almost without exception, are among the finest ever recovered in English tradition.

Dave Bordeway and Dave Young sang The White Hare in 2005 on their WildGoose CD Beer and Black Pudding. They noted:

Our rendition of The White Hare is from the singing of Martin Carthy, heard many years ago on a compilation LP. A celebration of the life and death of the mystical White Hare.

The Woodbine & Ivy Band sang White Hare on their 2015 CD Sleep On Sleeping On.

Jack Sharp sang White Hare on his 2020 album Good Times Older.
