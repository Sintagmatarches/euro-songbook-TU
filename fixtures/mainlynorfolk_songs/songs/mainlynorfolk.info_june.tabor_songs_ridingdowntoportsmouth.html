Although the present writers can trace no published versions of this song, its theme is, of course, a common one (see e.g. Ratcliffe Highway—Penguin Book of English Folk Songs). It is reported that a correspondent sent a version called Riding Down to Pochemar to Cecil Sharp, but the manuscript is now missing.

The tune, a major one, is sung in a very vagarious manner. The vagaries are consistent, however, and are clearly not the product of tentative semi-improvisations as they might at first seem.
