The Hills of Shiloh

[ Roud - ; DT HILSHILO ; Mudcat 17751 ; Shel Silverstein, Jim Friedman]

Shel Silverstein and Jim Friedman wrote The Hills of Shiloh in 1963.

Barry Skinner sang The Hills of Shiloh in 1971 on his Argo album Bed, Battle & Booze. He noted:

I first heard this haunting song, many years ago, sung by Paul McNeill one of the best of all contemporary ‘anti-war’ songs, and enhanced here by the superb flute playing of Martin Jenkins.

June Tabor sang The Hills of Shiloh in 2011 on her second album with the Oysterband, Ragged Kingdom.

Martin Simpson sang Hills of Shiloh in 2019 on his Topic album Rooted. He noted:

From 1977 to 1988, I worked with June Tabor. We performed a very wide selection of material including a set of songs from, or inspired by, the American Civil War. June performed Shel Silversteinâs Hills of Shiloh in that set, a song which portrays a broken woman whose partner has been killed in the war, a fact that she cannot accept after 40 years.

More Maids sang Hills of Shiloh on their 2021 CD Fourmaids. They noted:

A woman is waiting for her husband to return from the American Civil War, unable to accept the fact that the war has been over for forty years. A grim reflection of the aftermaths of the war.
