Gal With the Blue Dress On

[ Roud 7498 ; Mudcat 115952 , 145654 ; trad.]

Stan Hugill: Shanties From the Seven Seas

Emma Shelton of Flag Pond, Tennessee, sang this minstrel song, Pretty Little Girl With a Blue Dress On, in a 1950 recording on the Musical Traditions anthology of historic recordings of Appalachian singer and musicians, When Cecil Left the Mountains. The Roud index gives both this song and Gal With the Blue Dress On the same number 7498.

Stan Hugill wrote in Shanties From the Seven Seas (p. 197):

The following hauling song, Gal With the Blue Dress, is the one from which a similar line in Johnny, Come Down to Hilo was probably taken. I had it from Harding, who considered it one of the best in his repertoire for halyards. It is essentially a Negro song, probably one used by cotton hoosiers. I feel that L.A. Smith芒聙聶s Slapandergosheka is a similar or related song. Davis & Tozer give a version calling it ‘pumps’, which of course it could have been, but this appears to be a rather too poetical effort for hairy shellbacks or Negroes to have sung. There does exist a [Negro] minstrel song called The Gal With the Blue Dress On.

I worked with story teller Janet Dowling, who went to care homes and day centres to gather reminiscences from the residents or members. She then told selected stories to yearr 5 pupils in nearby schools, with whom I led workshops to create songs, using traditional tunes. When this story emerged, of Muriel, who had gone dancing in a blue dress, the choice of tune was obvious, and the 9 and 10 year old pupils of Sandy Lane Jnr School created lyrics in a few lunchtime workshops. The song needed very little further development to make it suitable for an adult voice.
