Lachlan Tigers

[ Roud 38607 ; AFS 53 ; Ballad Index FaE136 ; DT LCHLNTIG ; trad.]

A.L. Lloyd sang Lachlan Tigers in 1956 on his Riverside LP Australian Bush Songs, and in 1958 on his Wattle album Across the Western Plains. Martyn Wyndham-Read sang Lachlan Tigers in 1966 on his W&G album Australian Songs and in 1973 on his Argo album Harry the Hawker Is Dead.

Danny Spooner sang Lachlan Tigers in 1978 on his and Gordon McIntyreâs Larrikin album Revived and Relieved!. And he and Martyn Wyndham-Read sang it in 1989 on their album All Around Down Under. They noted:

Alex Cumming and Nicola Beazley sang Lachlan Tigers on their 2016 CD Across the Water. They noted:

A great traditional Australian folk song about sheep shearing in New South Wales. The Lachlan river runs through some of the best areas for raising sheep in the Western part of New South Wales.
