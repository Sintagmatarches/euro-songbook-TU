Ralph Vaughan Williams collected Lovely Joan in 1908 from Christopher Jay, Acle, Norfolk [VWML RVW2/4/36] , and later published it in The Penguin Book of English Folk Songs. A.L. Lloyd recorded Lovely Joan in 1960 for A Selection From the Penguin Book of English Folk Songs. As all tracks from this LP it was reissued in 2003 on the CD England & Her Traditional Songs. Lloyd noted on the album:

The words of this skittish song have hitherto been published only in modified form. Itâs easy to scoff at the bowdlerising habits of early collectors, but it must be remembered that, half a century ago, folk song was generally considered to be of doubtful value, and collectors were under pressure to compromise, if they were going to get the tunes heard at all. The rather intricate moral issues suggested by Lovely Joan delighted country audiences, as they amuse us now; but many powerful folk to whom the collectors looked for support would have found the authentic text deeply shocking. Several tunes go to Lovely Joan, none of them duds. This one was sung to Vaughan Williams in a Norfolk pub by a labourer named Christopher Jay. Later, the composer wove the tune into his well-known Fantasia on Greensleeves.
