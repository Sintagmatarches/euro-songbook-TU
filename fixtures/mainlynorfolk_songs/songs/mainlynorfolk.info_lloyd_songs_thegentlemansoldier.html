The Gentleman Soldier

[ Roud 178 / Song Subject MAS1506 ; Master title: The Gentleman Soldier ; G/D 7:1471 ; Ballad Index VWL040 ; VWML AGG/8/27 ; Bodleian Roud 178 ; DT SNTRYBOX ; Mudcat 310 , 18107 ; trad.]

A.G. Gilchrist collected The Gentleman Soldier in 1907 from Thomas Coomber, Blackham, Sussex [VWML AGG/8/27] . Ralph Vaughan Williams and A.L. Lloyd published it in The Penguin Book of English Folk Songs. A.L. Lloyd recorded The Gentleman Soldier in 1960 for A Selection From the Penguin Book of English Folk Songs. Like all tracks from this LP it was reissued in 2003 on his CD England & Her Traditional Songs. Lloyd noted:

Kipling knew this from the army in India, and he quoted a fragment in Soldiers Three. Mr. Coomber of Blackham, Sussex, who gave the song to Miss A.G. Gilchrist in 1907, had learnt it in an army camp. The tune has a military character; it sounds as if it is founded on bugle calls, and would make a first-class regimental march. However, it is very doubtful that it was originally an army tune. Scottish farm-hands know it as Drumdelgie. In Ireland it is attached to a comic song, Cassidy Brought Me Home, while the Welsh have it as a romantic piece, Dydd Llun y Boreu. It was probably a dance-tune to start with. A “gentleman soldier” is one of a yeomanry regiment.

Martin Carthy recorded Gentleman Soldier with Dave Swarbrick in 1967 for their album Byker Hill; it was also included on their compilation album Selections. He noted on the original album:

Gentleman Soldier is an example of the husband芒聙聶s infidelity seeming unimportant. People in countries occupied by the British for centuries may well have wished that her soldiery had acted in a similar way to this guard more often; indeed stories and legends abound in which the attention of a guard is diverted by a beautiful woman with drastic consequences. The lady in this song, however, seems to have no altruistic motives; she seemed to be minding her own business. Collected in Sussex.

The Ripley Wayfarers sang The Gentleman Soldier in 1972 on their Traditional Sound Recordings album Five Wells. This track was also included in 2002 on the Fellside anthology of the soldier in song from the English Civil War to the Falklands, Enlist for a Soldier.

Jack the Lad sang Gentleman Soldier in 1975 on their Charisma album Rough Diamonds.

Steeleye Span performed Gentleman Soldier to the tune of The British Grenadiers during their 1991 tour. A live recording was released on their 1992 CD Tonight芒聙聶s the Night. John Tams and Roger Wilson sang Gentleman Soldier in 1996 on Over the Hills and Far Away: The Music of Sharpe.

Ex-Liverpool Spinner Mick Groves sang Gentleman Soldier on his 2010 album Still Spinning. He noted:

We started The Spinners Folk Club in Liverpool in October 1958, Gentleman Soldier was one of the 13 traditional songs I had learntfor opening night. I cannot recall how it went down fifty years ago, but this time I am ably assisted by Jackie Oates, a fine young singer and fiddle player.

Jon Boden sang Gentleman Soldier as the 14 April 2011 entry of his project A Folk Song a Day.

Andy Turner heard The Gentleman Soldier first on Byker Hill and learned it then from The Penguin Book of English Folk Songs. He sang it as the 16 August 2014 entry of his project A Folk Song a Week.

The Gilchrist Collective sang The Gentleman Soldier on their 2022 album of English folk songs collected by Anne Gilchrist, Most Truly Yours. They noted:

A song which became very popular after it was included in the first edition of the The Penguin Book of English Folk Songs (1959). This version was sung in 1907 by Mr Thomas Coomber [VWML AGG/8/27] who had probably learnt it at army camp. Thomas was a railway labourer before becoming a dairyman and, although illiterate, had memorised some quite long texts. On Sundays he would sit in his armchair with the newspaper and say: “I芒聙聶m reading the pictures”.
