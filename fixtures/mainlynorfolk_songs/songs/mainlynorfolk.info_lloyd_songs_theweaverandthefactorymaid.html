Alistair Hulett sang The Weaver and the Factory Maid on his 2001 album In Sleepy Scotland. He noted

The invention of the steam shuttle in 1764 revolutionised the textile industry. The ancient craft of handloom weaving was transformed from a well respected cottage industry into a desperate struggle for survival. Handloom weavers were driven down past the point of destitution and many fought back by breaking into factory mills to smash the machines that were turning them into beggars. This love song, learned from the singing of A.L. Lloyd, is rendered all the more poignant by understanding the social conditions in which it was made.
