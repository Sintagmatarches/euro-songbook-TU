The Dreadnought

[ Roud 924 ; Laws D13 ; Henry H194 ; Ballad Index LD13 ; DT DREDNGHT ; Mudcat 166643 ; trad.]

The version of this song in Sam Henry芒聙聶s Songs of the People is called The Zared and dated 30 July 1927. Joanna C. Colcord says in Songs of American Sailormen (1938) that The Dreadnought芒聙聶s tune is “a variant of an old English naval song, The Flash Frigate, or La Pique.” Both the Henry and the Colcord versions do not yet have the Derry Down chorus.

Ewan MacColl sang The Dreadnought on his, A.L. Lloyd and Harry H. Corbett芒聙聶s 1954/5 Topic album The Singing Sailor. This track was also included on their albums Row Bullies Row (1957), Chants de Marins Anglais No 1 (probably 1957), Singing Sailors (1957), Off to Sea Once More (1958), and The Coast of Peru (1963) and on the anthologies Sea Songs and Shanties (Topic Sampler No 7, 1971), Chants de Marins IV (1984) and Sailors芒聙聶 Songs & Sea Shanties (2004). The last album芒聙聶s booklet noted:

There have been few songs made to celebrate the qualities of particular ships but The Dreadnought is an exception. It pays homage to the famous Western Ocean packet built at Maryport, Mass, in 1853. Though not the fastest of her class, she could stand any amount of hard driving in hard weather and once ran from Rock Light to Sandy Hook in 19 days.

Martin Carthy sang The Dreadnought on the Hullabaloo ABC Television programme broadcast on 30 November 1963.

A recording of Barbara Dickson singing The Dreadnought live in between 1969 and 1973 was included in 2013 on her folk club album B4 Seventy-Four.

Tom Sullivan sang The Dreadnought on the 2004 Folkways anthology Classic Maritime Music.

The Belgian band Kadril sang The Dreadnought on their 2005 album De Andere Kust.

Louis Killen sang The Dreadnought in 1970 on his South Street Seaport Museum album of songs of the Cape Horn Road, 50 South to 50 South, in 1973 on the National Geographic Society anthology Songs & Sounds of the Sea, and in 1995 on his CD Sailors, Ships & Chanteys. The last recording was also included in 2004 on the Lancaster Maritime Festival anthology Beware of the Press-Gang!!. He commented in his album芒聙聶s notes:

Launched in 1853, this packet was renowned for its fast passages between Liverpool and New York under the command of Sam Samuels. Captain Samuels was a colourful character who published an autobiography extolling his exploits as a bully skipper.

Jack Rutter et al sang The Dreadnought (Bound Away) in 2022 on the Topic anthology Sea Song Sessions.

The “derry down” chorus is also used in the song The Old Bachelor (Roud 7162, G/D 7:1390) which was collected by Gavin Greig from John Quirrie in ca 1906.
