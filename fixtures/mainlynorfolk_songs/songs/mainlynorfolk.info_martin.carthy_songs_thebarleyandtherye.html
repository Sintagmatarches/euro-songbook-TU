The Barley and the Rye

[ Roud 23268 ; Master title: The Barley and the Rye ; DT BARLRYE ; Mudcat 22034 ; trad.]

Martin Carthy sang The Barley and the Rye in 1965 on his first album Martin Carthy. He noted:

E.J. Moeran collected it from him more than forty years ago. The tune is a handsome member of the vast Dives and Lazarus family; the well-known sailor song Rounding the Horn or The Gallant Frigate Amphitrite is its cousin.

The Barley and The Rye is a song which produces a deceptively sly philosophy from a notoriously difficult marital situation. The arrangement here is the one used by Lea Nicholson and myself for some live performances in 1972.

Bill Jones sang The Barley and the Rye on her 2001 album Panchpuran.
