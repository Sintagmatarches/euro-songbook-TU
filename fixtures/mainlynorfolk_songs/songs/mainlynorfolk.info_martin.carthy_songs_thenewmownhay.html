(Among) The New-Mown Hay

[ Roud 2941 ; Ballad Index K184 ; VWML CJS2/9/1160 , SBG/1/2/848 ; Folkinfo 127 ; DT MORNDEW4 ; Mudcat 6177 ; trad.]

Sabine Baring-Gould, Henry Fleetwood Sheppard: Songs of the West Cecil J. Sharp, George B. Gardiner: Folk-Songs of England, Book III: Folk-Songs From Hampshire Collected by George B. Gardiner

See also the related song The Baffled Knight (Roud 11; Child 112) which basically has the same plot.

Cecil Sharp recorded probably Alfred Edgell of Chew Magna, Somerset, on 26 December 1907 singing Among the New-Mown Hay [VWML CJS2/9/1160] . This recording was included in 1998 on the EFDSS anthology A Century of Song.

William Rew of Sidbury, Devon, sang The New-Mown Hay to Peter Kennedy and Wyn Humphries on 1954. This recording was included in 2000 on the Rounder anthology Songs of Seduction but not on the previous Caedmon/Topic LP from the Folk Songs of Britain series on which it is based. Martin Carthy sang The New-Mown Hay on his and Dave Swarbrickâs 1992 album Skin and Bone. He noted:

Percy Grainger ought in fact to have opened something of a can of worms when he started using recording techniques in his song gathering, but others, after tinkering with the idea and making a few recordings, put the lid back on and the can back in the cupboard. The Cecil Sharp Centenary Collective sang Among the New-Mown Hay in 2003 on their CD As I Cycled Out on a May Morning.
