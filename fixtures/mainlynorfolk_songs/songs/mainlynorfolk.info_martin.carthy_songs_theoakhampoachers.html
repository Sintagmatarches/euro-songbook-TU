Wiggy (1926-2001) was a respected custodian of his Romany roots, who said the song related to an incident involving his own family. Although a version of the song first appeared in 1819, Gwilym Davies suggests (on glostrad.com) that it has been linked to a trial (held in Oakham) in 1833 of three Perkins brothers who shot and wounded gamekeeper Thomas Peach whilst poaching. John was hung, Robert died on a prison barge on the river Thames and George was transported to Tasmania.
