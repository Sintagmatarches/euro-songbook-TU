The Wren / The King / Joy, Health, Love and Peace

[ Roud 32955 / Song Subject MAS1481 ; Ballad Index FO059 ; DT WRENSONG , WRENSN2 ; Mudcat 28750 ; trad.]

“The king was the wren. The wren was the king of the birds. In ancient religions the king was sacrificed every seven years for the fertility and good of the tribe. In some places (Ireland) the queen was royal and married new consorts to be sacrificed. The consort was treated well for seven years (or one year) and then sacrificed by the new consort. A wren was killed and dressed up in ribbons, etc. and carried around the village. This is from Pembrokeshire in South Wales, commemorating the wren-killing on St Stephenâs Day, 26 December. “Old Christmas”, still celebrated rather than 25 December is Twelfth Night.”
— Digital Tradition

Liam Clancy sang The Wran Song to collector Diane Hamilton in summer 1955. This recording was included in 1956 on the Tradition album of folk songs and dances from the Irish countryside, The Lark in the Morning.

Martin Carthy and Dave Swarbrick recorded The Wren in 1969 for their duo album Prince Heathen. This track was also included in 2006 on the Fellside anthology Midwinter. Carthy noted:

Collected by Andy Nisbet, formerly of Swansea University, from two old ladies in Pembrokeshire.
