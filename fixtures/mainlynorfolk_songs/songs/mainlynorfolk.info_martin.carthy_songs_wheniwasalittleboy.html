John Stickle of Unst, Shetland, sang the boasting ballad When I Was a Little Boy to Patrick Shuldham-Shaw in February 1952 (BBC recording 18623). This track was later included on the anthology Songs of Animals and Other Marvels (The Folk Songs of Britain Volume 10; Caedmon 1961; Topic 1970). Ralph Vaughan Williams and A.L. Lloyd included it in 1959 in The Penguin Book of English Folk Songs.

Dave Goulder and Liz Dyer sang When I Was a Little Boy in 1970 on their Argo album January Man.

Cyril Tawney sang A Lying Tale in 1970 on his Argo album Cyril Tawney Sings Childrenâs Songs From Devon and Cornwall.

Roy Harris sang When I Was a Little Boy on his 1975 LP Champions of Folly. Martin Carthy played guitar on this track and Bobby Campbell played mandola.

Gordon Tyrrall sang When I Was a Little Boy on his 1993 album The Bridge Flows. He noted:

Noted down in 1947 from the singing of John Stickle of Shetland.

Martin Carthy and Dave Swarbrick sang When I Was a Little Boy on their 2006 album Straws in the Wind. A live performance from 10 March 2024 was released in the same year on the Broadside Hacks cassette A Tribute to Les Cousins. Carthy noted on his album:

Robert Leng with Jossy ‘Pop’ Mainprize and Jim Eldon sang A Thunnerinâ Sort of a Lie in three recordings made in Flamborough in 1988 and at BBC Radio Humberside in April 1989. These were include in 2018 on their Musical Traditions anthology Songs and Tales From Flamborough Head. They noted:

Anna Tam sang When I Was a Little Girl on her 2022 album Hatching Hares. She noted:

In the Penguin Book of English Folk Songs as When I Was a Little Boy; the fourth verse of this exuberant nonsense song gives the album its title.

A variant called As I Set Off to Turkey was printed in Frank Purslowâs 1960 book The Everlasting Circle. Jo Freya sang As I Set Off to Turkey in 1992 on her Saydisc album Traditional Songs of England. The liner notes commented:

Collected by George Gardiner from David Marlow of Basingstoke, 1906 [VWML GG/1/10/568] . This is a hyperbolic song of the The Derby Ram genre and Gardiner says that it was current among young people about 1830-40 when Mr Marlow was a boy.
