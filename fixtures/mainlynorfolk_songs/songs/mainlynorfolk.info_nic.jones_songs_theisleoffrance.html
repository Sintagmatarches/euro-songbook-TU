‘The Isle of France’ refers to Mauritius, one of the Mascarene Islands that lie in the Indian Ocean east of Madagascar. In 1598 the Dutch Second Fleet to the Spice Islands, blown off course, discovered the island and named it in honour of Prince Maurits van Nassau, then Stadtholder of the Netherlands.
