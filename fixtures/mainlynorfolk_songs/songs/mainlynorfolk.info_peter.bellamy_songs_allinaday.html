All in a Day

[Alex Glasgow]

Alex Glasgow sang his own song All in a Day in the 1970s BBC 2 television series The Camera and the Song and on the accompanying soundtrack album from 1975.

Peter Bellamy sang All in a Day on his 1975 LP Tell It Like It Was. He accompanied himself on concertina; Chris Birch and Anthea Bellamy provided harmony vocals. He noted:

The high-point of the recent BBC 2 series The Camera and the Song was an episode centred on Alex Glasgowâs excellent songs reflecting life in the North-East. This song was the high-point of that episode, where it was used to illustrate the launching and christening of a new craft from the Tyne-side shipyards, and the strange celebratory ritual which traditionally excludes the craftsmen whose labours made the event possible.

Bob Fox sang All in a Day in 2006 on his Topic CD The Blast.

The Unthanks sang All in a Day on their 2012 CD Songs From the Shipyards (Diversions Vol. 3).
