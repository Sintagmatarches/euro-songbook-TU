Dives and Lazarus

[ Roud 477 ; Child 56 ; Ballad Index C056 ; Bodleian Roud 477 ; GlosTrad Roud 477 ; Folkinfo 62 ; DT DIVRSLAZ , DIVRSLA2 ; Mudcat 47438 ; trad.]

Dives and Lazarus is a story told by Jesus in the Gospel of Luke (Luke 16:19-31). It tells of an unnamed rich man and a poor beggar named Lazarus. In the Latin Bible, the unnamed rich man is referred to as Dives from dives, the Latin word for rich.

There are a number of broadside texts at Bodleian Library Broadsides, including four printed in Birmingham.
