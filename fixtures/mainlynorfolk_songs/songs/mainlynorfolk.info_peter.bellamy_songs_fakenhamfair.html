Fakenham Fair

[ Roud - ; Mudcat 90963 ; trad., possibly Peter Bellamy]

Peter Bellamy sang Fakenham Fair unaccompanied in 1968 on his first solo LP, Mainly Norfolk. He noted:

Yarmouth Town and Fakenham Fair, straight forward Good-Time songs, were both learned from Pete Bullen of Norwich who had them from his grandfather.

Tim Laycock sang Fakenham Fair in 1976 on the fundraiser album, The Second Folk Review Record. The sleeve notes commented:

With Yarmouth Town, Peter Bellamy learned this song from Peter Bullen; neither has been collected anywhere else.

Damien Barber sang Fakenham Fair on his 1991 cassette Blass Me!.

Bellowhead recorded Fakenham Fair for their 2008 CD Matachin. This track was also included on the BBC Radio 2 Folk Awards 2009 compilation and on Bellowheadâs 2015 anthology Pandemonium. A live performance from at O2 Academy, Bournemouth on 2 May 2011 was released on their 2011 DVD Hedonism Live. Jon Boden noted on the first album:

The origins of this song are somewhat murky. Peter Bellamy learnt it from a Peter Bullen of Norwich, who learnt it from his grandfather. There are, as far as we know, no other collected versions of the song so it seems likely it was unique to the Bullen family. The language seems relatively modern leading some to suggest that Bullenâs grandfather may have been the composer. There are other more sinister conspiracy theories but we donât go in for that sort of thing here at Bellowhead HQ.

Jon Boden also sang Fakenham Fair as the 7 September 2010 entry of his project A Folk Song a Day.

Anna Tam sang Fakenham Fair on her 2022 album Hatching Hares. She noted:

Another ‘fair song’, this one in Norfolk, and a straightfonward tale of love and lust in my favourite time—a 5/4 waltz—which works perfectly with the lyrics and story—bouncy, fun and a little off kilter. Collected by Peter Bellamy, from Peter Bullen of Norwich, who had it from his grandfather.
