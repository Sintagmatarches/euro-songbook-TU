Fire Marengo / Fire Maringo

[ Roud - ; Ballad Index Hugi016 ; DT FIRMRING ; Mudcat 63103 ; trad.]

The Young Tradition recorded four sea shanties, Fire Maringo, Hanging Johnny, Bring âem Down, and Haul on the Bowline, for their 1967 EP Chicken on a Raft. Royston Wood sang lead on the first and third shanty, Peter Bellamy on the second and fourth. All tracks from the EP were included in the compilation album The Young Tradition Sampler and CD Galleries / Chicken on a Raft / No Relation. The EP sleeve notes commented about Fire Maringo:

Royston was cruising through Hugillâs fine book of sea songs and shanties [Shanties From the Seven Seas, p. 16] when he found a fragment of an old cotton screwing worksong. There was no tune attached, and only four verses, so he added some floating verses and made a tune for it. Cotton screwing was about the hardest shipboard task there was: the bales of cotton were forced into the hold until they were packed solid. The men who worked at this would be shorebound sailors, working in the South American harbours till they had saved themselves some money; all nations were represented, so the songs they sang would perhaps contain references to Sally Brownâs counterpart in all parts of the world; for the purposes of this song, the sailor came from Liverpool.

Johnny Collins with Dave Webber and Pete Watkinson sang the hauling shanty Fire Marengo on their 1996 album Shanties & Songs of the Sea.

Ian Giles, John Spiers, Jon Boden and Graham Metcalfe sang Fire Maringo on the 2002 Gift of Music album Sea Shanties.

Bellowhead sang this shanty as Fire Marengo on their 2006 CD Burlesque and 2009 DVD Live at Shepherds Bush Empire. Jon Boden also sang Fire Marengo as the 24 November 2010 entry of his project A Folk Song a Day, and in 2022 on the Topic album Sea Song Sessions.

John Jones sang Fire Marengo in 2009 on his Westpark album Rising Road. He noted:

I first heard this song by the Young Tradition many years ago. I have added verses from A.L. Lloydâs book Folk Song in England.

The Salts sang Fire Marengo on their 2018 CD Brave. A live recording from Wiltonâs Music Hall, London, on 13 April 2023, was included in the following year on their album Live in London Town.
