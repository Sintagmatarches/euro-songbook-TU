The Santa Fe Trail

[ Roud 5096 ; Ballad Index Ohr085 ; Mudcat 17944 ; James Grafton Rogers (1911)]

Jolly Jack sang The Santa Fe Trail in 1988 on their Fellside album A Long Time Travelling. They noted:

Riding alongside the schooner or covered wagon, in which her parents travelled, this bronzed vision is a far cry from the reality of the frontier town ‘ladies’.

Ian Robb sang The Santa Fe Trail on his 1994 CD From Different Angels. He noted:

The Santa Fe Trail is an ersatz but irresistibly charming cowboy song written in 1911 by a lawyer, James Grafton Rogers, who later became a big wheel in the depression era Hoover administration. He should probably have stuck to writing songs. Learned from Peter [Bellamy], with bits from Utah芒聙聶s Deseret String Band.

Brian Peters and Gordon Tyrrall sang The Santa Fe Trail in 2000 on their duo CD The Moving Moon. They noted:

The late and much missed Peter Bellamy used to sing The Santa Fe Trail, haranguing audiences if they failed to hit the harmonies he thought essential. We did our best!

Jon Boden sang Santa Fe Trail as the 22 October 2010 entry of his project A Folk Song a Day.

Damien Barber and Mike Wilson sang Santa Fe Trail in 2011 on their CD The Old Songs.

Ollie King sang Santa Fe Trail in 2014 on his RootBeat album Gambit. He noted:

This somewhat uncharacteristically bluesy arrangement came out of an afternoon of procrastination during my A levels.

Hannah Sanders and Ben Savage sang The Santa Fe Trail in 2018 on their Sungrazing album Awake.
