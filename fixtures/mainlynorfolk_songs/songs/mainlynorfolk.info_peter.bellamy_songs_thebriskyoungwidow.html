Jack Crawford sang A Brisk Young Widow in 2008 on his WildGoose CD Pride of the Season. He noted:

The only known example of this song in the oral tradition was collected by Cecil Sharp from George Radford at Bridgwater Union Workhouse, Somerset, on 22 August 1905. Mr Radford was 76 at the time, and died less than a year later. According to Maud Karpeles he told Sharp that his father, Job Radford, had been a great singer but that this was the only song he had managed to learn from him. It came to me through the singing of the late, lamented Royston Wood.

Jon Boden sang The Brisk Young Widow as the 29 September 2010 entry of his project A Folk Song a Day.

James Findlay sang The Brisk Young Widow in 2012 on his second Fellside CD, Another Day Another Story. He noted:

“As the tune is a good one and the words are spirited, the song seems worth printing.” (Cecil Sharp).
Collected from George Radford in 1905 at Bridgwater Union, Somerset, [VWML CJS2/10/582] , a shoemaker from East Brent, and a bachelor with no children to support him in his old age. George learnt this song from his father, “a great singer”. Peter Bellamy recorded the definitive version of this song in my eyes. He brought the song to life with his phrasings and expressions. Peter sang this song as a tribute to his good friend and fellow band member Royston Wood.

Alex Cumming sang Brisk Young Widow on their 2020 download album Isolation Sessions: The Songs. They noted:

This song was collection from George Radford in Bridgwater by Cecil Sharp. Sharp wrote: “He said his father was a good singer but he could never learn any of his songs, except one… Brisk Young Widow.”
