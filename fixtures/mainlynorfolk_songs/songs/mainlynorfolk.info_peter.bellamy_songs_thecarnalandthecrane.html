Nowell Sing We Clear sang The Carnal and the Crane and King Herod and the Cock in 1985 on their third album, Nowell Sing We Clear Vol. 3.

Jo Freya sang The Carnal and the Crane in 1992 on her Saydisc album Traditional Songs of England. The liner notes commented:

Coope Boyes & Simpson sang The Carnal and the Crane in 1998 on their No Masters CD A Garland of Carols.

The New Scorpion Band sang The Carnal and the Crane as the title track of their 2001 CD The Carnal and the Crane. They noted:

Our arrangement uses oboe, cor anglais, flute, viola and laouto.

Maddy Prior and the Carnival Band recorded The Carnal and the Crane in 2001 for their CD Gold Frankincense & Myrhh. This track was also included in 2012 on their compilation album A Christmas Caper. They also sang it some years later live at Oxford Town Hall. A recording of this concert was published in November 2005 on the CD and DVD An Evening of Carols and Capers.

Lyrics

Peter Bellamy sings The Carnal and the Crane

As I passed by a river side,
And there as I did reign,
In argument I chanced to hear
A carnal and a crane.

The carnal said unto the crane,
“If all the world should turn,
Before we had the Father,
But now we have the Son!”

“From whence does the Son come,
From where and from what place?”
He said, “In a manger,
Between an ox and ass.”

“I pray thee,” said the carnal,
“Tell me before thou go,
Was not the mother of Jesus Christ
Conceivâd by the Holy Ghost?”

She was the purest virgin,
And the cleanest from all sin;
She was the handmaid of our Lord
And the mother of our king.”

“Where is the golden cradle
That Christ was rocked in?
And where are the silken sheets
That Jesus was wrapt in?”

“A manger was the cradle
That Christ was rocked in.
The provender the asses left
So sweetly he slept on.”
