A few years later, in 1973, Roger Watson recorded Watercress-O himself with his group Muckram Wakes for their first album, A Map of Derbyshire. He noted:

The stimuli for writing Watercress-O and Gilliver came from my late grandparents, Joe and Sally Clarke. Watercress-O, written in 1965, was inspired by my grandmother芒聙聶s recollections of a watercress seller, who had visited the back-to-back houses of the mining village where she had lived as a young women. My grandfather had been a typical son of a mining family; he left school at the age of twelve and worked at the colliery until he retired. Gilliver is taken from a story which he recounted as being one of his earliest memories of the pit; ganging (pony driving) was his first job, and Gilliver was his first pony. It appears that the miners were either very cruel or very kind to their horses, and the horses were wise enough to reward kindness if the opportunity came. The song was written in 1966—Colin Cater wrote the tune.

The choir Freshly Ground sang Watercress-O in 2013 on their WildGoose CD The Good Red Earth. Issy Emeney noted:

Roger [Watson] wrote this cracking song in 1965. “The child was my mother, my grandmother told me the story. Gathering and selling watercress kept many people in a small extra income around our North Nottinghamshire mining community. Children listened out for the watercress seller on Sunday afternoon as keenly as kids today do for the ice cream van. The strike referred to was c. 1920 when my mother was 6.”

Sound Tradition sang New Oysters and Watercress-O in 2014 on their Treewind Studio album Blackbird and in 2026 on WildGoose芒聙聶s anthology of songs by Roger Watson, All in Due Curse. Roger Watson noted:

Sixty years ago, my grandmother told me about the watercress seller who used to cry his wares in the street where she lived in the years after the First World War. Taking the street cry as a chorus, I began making a song. Some weeks later, my mother told me that the watercress seller went out of business during the strikes of the early 1920s, giving rise to the rest of the song. Sound Tradition have recognised that early origin by arranging the song together with an arrangement of other street cries made by Thomas Ravenscroft in the early seventeenth century.

Stick in the Wheel sang Watercress-O on their 2024 album A Thousand Pokes.
