Dark As a Dungeon (Down in the Mine)

[ Roud 6392 ; Ballad Index LoF155 ; DT DARKDUNG ; Mudcat 98582 ; Merle Travis]

Dark As a Dungeon is a song written by singer-songwriter Merle Travis first released in 1946, lamenting the danger and drudgery of being a coal miner in a shaft mine.

Steve Benbow, Shirley Bland and Jimmie Macgregor sang Merle Travis芒聙聶 song Dark As a Dungeon in 1960 at Cecil Sharp House, London, in a recording made by Peter Kennedy. This was published on the HMV 10" LP Rocket Along: New Ballads on Old Lines. Kennedy noted:

Steve Benbow, now one of the most outstanding of modern British folk-singers, accompanies himself with the plectrum guitar. The song was composed by the American guitarist, Merle Travis, who is himself a miner.

The Reivers sang Down in the Mines on their 1960 EP The Work of The Reivers Volume 2. The liner notes commented:

The Reivers, while basing themselves on Scottish material, draw upon a world-wide repertoire. This song was written by Merle Travis and it first appeared in a collection from his native Kentucky called Folk Songs of the Hills. Incidentally, in the same album was his Sixteen Tons.

Alex Campbell with Sandy Denny and others in the background sang Down in the Mine on his 1967 LP Alex Campbell and His Friends.
