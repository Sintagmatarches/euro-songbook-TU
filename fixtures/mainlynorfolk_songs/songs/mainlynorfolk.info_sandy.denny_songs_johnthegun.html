John the Gun

[Sandy Denny]

Sandy Denny recorded several versions of John the Gun, which is one of her best known songs:

  1. The original recordings in late 1970 for the abortive second Fotheringay album finally found their way on the 2008 CD Fotheringay 2.
  2. A Fotheringay live performance at Grugahalle, Essen, Germany, on 23 October 1970 was included in 2011 on the concert recording Essen 1970.
  3. A Fotheringay performance filmed for “Beat Club” of Radio Bremen, Germany, in October 1970 wasnât actually broadcast on that programme. It was included in 2015 on the DVD of Fotheringayâs Universal anthology Nothing More.
  4. A Fotheringay performance on BBC Radio “Sound of the Seventies”, hosted by Bob Harris, recorded on 15 November 1970 and broadcast on 21 December, was included in 2015 on Nothing More too.
  5. Sandyâs studio version appeared in 1971 on her first solo album, The North Star Grassman and the Ravens, and was included in her 2CD anthology No More Sad Refrains. Sandy was accompanied by Richard Thompson, electric guitar; Pat Donaldson, bass; Gerry Conway, drums; Ian Whiteman, piano; Trevor Lucas, acoustic guitar and vocals; Barry Dransfield, violin and vocals; Royston Wood and Robin Dransfield, vocals.   6. A live version recorded at the Paris Theatre, London, on 16 March 1972 for BBC “Radio 1 in Concert” and broadcast on 25 March 1972 was released in 1997 on The BBC Sessions 1971-73 and in 2007 on the 3CD+DVD set Live at the BBC.
  7. The Live at the BBC box set contains a further live recording from November 1972 for the BBC Radio 1 show “Sounds on Sunday”, hosted by Johnny Moran, and broadcast on 3 December 1972.
  8. A Fairport Convention live version (with Donahue, Lucas, Mattacks, Pegg and Swarbrick) was recorded on 26 January 1974 at the Sydney Opera House, Australia and appeared in the same year on Fairportâs Live album and in 1986 on Sandyâs Who Knows Where the Time Goes box set.

  10. A third Fairport live performance from May 1974 at Ebbets Field, Denver, Colorado was released in 2002 on the bonus CD on Before the Moon.
  11. A live recording probably from 31 October 1975 at Brunel University, Uxbridge is on the 2004 Talking Elephant CD Who Knows? The Woodworm Archives Vol. 1.
