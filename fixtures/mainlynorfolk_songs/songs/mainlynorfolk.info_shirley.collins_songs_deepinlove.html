Cyril Tawney sang A Ship Came Sailing, as collected by Sabine Baring-Gould from W. Nichols in Whitchurch, Devon on 29 May 1891 [VWML SBG/2/3/32] , in 1963 on his EFDSS album Between Decks. He noted:

This must have been one of the first songs that I learned from the Baring-Gould collection. I was to discover later, of course, that these stanzas crop up time and time again, often in other songs as floating verses. I particularly like singing it because it contains a form of the “I wish, I wish” stanza that can be sung by a man without blushing!

Cyril Tawney also sang Must I Be Bound?, as collected by H.E.D. Hammond collected in August 1905 from Jacob Baker of Bere Regis, Dorset [VWML HAM/2/5/15] , in 1973 on his Argo album of traditional love songs from South West England, I Will Give My Love.
