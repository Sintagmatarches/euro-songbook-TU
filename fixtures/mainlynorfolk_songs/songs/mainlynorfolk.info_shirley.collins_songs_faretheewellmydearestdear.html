Fare Thee Well, My Dearest Dear

[ Roud 1035 / Song Subject MAS942 ; Ballad Index VWL038 ; VWML RVW2/2/202 ; Mudcat 18105 ; trad.]

James Reeves: The Everlasting Circle Ralph Vaughan Williams and A.L. Lloyd: Penguin Book of English Folk Songs

This sad parting song was collected by Ralph Vaughan Williams in 1904 from Mrs Harriet Verrall of Monxgate near Horsham, Sussex, and published in his and A.L. Lloydâs Penguin Book of English Folk Songs.

Carolyn Hester sang Fare The Well on the 1963 Hullabaloo ABC Television programme broadcast on 5 October 1963.

The Trugs sang Fare Thee Well in 1971 on their Traditional Sound Recordings album And Boldly Go to Sea.

Shirley and Dolly Collins recorded Fare Thee Well, My Dearest Dear in 1976 for their album Amaranth. This track was also included in their anthology Within Sound. There are also two live recordings available, one from Dublin 1978 can be found on the CD Harking Back, the other from the Folk Festival Sidmouth 1979 on the CD Snapshots. Shirley Collins also sang Fare Thee Well, My Dearest Dear in 2023 on her Domino album Archangel Hill where she noted:

In 1904 in Monksgate, West Sussex, Ralph Vaughan Williams noted down this exquisite and poignant song from Mrs Harriet Verrall [VWML RVW2/2/202] , who, with her husband, Peter, sang over one hundred songs to RVW and other collectors. It is one of many narratives in which the female protagonist cuts her hair, dons male attire and stows away on board ship, but is the only one I know that has Venice as the proposed destination, and is likewise rare in rewarding such enterprise with an unhappy ending.
