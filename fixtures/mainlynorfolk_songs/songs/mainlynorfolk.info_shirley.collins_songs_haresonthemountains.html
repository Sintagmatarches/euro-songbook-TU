Horton Barker of Chilhowie, Smyth County, Virginia, sang Hares on the Mountain to Maud Karpeles in September 1950. This recording was included in 2007 on the Musical Traditions anthology of historic recordings of Appalachian singers and musicians 1927-1955, When Cecil Left the Mountains. Harry List of Sweffling, Suffolk, sang a composite of Hares on the Mountain and The Knife on the Window in a recording made by Peter Kennedy on 11 October 1953 that was included in 2014 on the Topic anthology The Barley Mow (The Voice of the People Volume 26).

Shirley Collins recorded Hares on the Mountain twice: in 1959 for her first LP Sweet England and in 1964 together with Davy Graham for their album Folk Roots, New Routes. On the latter version she used two introductory verses from the related song Sally My Dear / The Knife in the Window.
