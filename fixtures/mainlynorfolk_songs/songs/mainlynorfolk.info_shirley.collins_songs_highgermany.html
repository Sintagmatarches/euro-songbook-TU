There are three songs known as High Germany. This page describes the best known one; the second is also called The Two Lovers or True Lovers (Roud 1445) as sung by Tony Rose on his album Under the Greenwood Tree, the third is The Wars of Germany (Roud 5608).

Phoebe Smith sang Higher Germanie to Peter Kennedy at Melton, Woodbridge, Suffolk, on 8 July 1956. This recording was included in 1994 on the Saydisc anthology Songs of the Travelling People and in 2012 on the Topic anthology Iâm a Romany Rai (The Voice of the People Volume 22). Another recording of Phoebe Smith recorded by Paul Carter and Frank Purslow in her home in Melton, Woodbridge, Suffolk, in 1969 was included on her 1970 LP Once I Had a True Love, on the 1995 Topic anthology Hidden English, and in 2009 on Topicâs 70th anniversary anthology Three Score and Ten. The 2012 anthology quoted Phoebe Smith:

The family were really good singers and I learnt from those, you see, and all from memory—all the old people. I always wanted to be a singer, and I was always singing at home, working. Just listening to my father and mother. I think the old songs have much better tunes—and Iâve heard them sung quite different with different people. Father sang whenever he felt like it, you know, to all of us. If mother was out-of-sorts heâd say, “Come on, pretty Polly, Iâll take you for a walk. Shall we go picking cowslips?”

Shirley Collins learned Higher Germanie from Peter Kennedyâs field recording of Phoebe Smith and sang it on the album recorded in 1959 by—again—Peter Kennedy, A Jug of Punch, which recording was also included on her 2002 anthology Within Sound. She recorded it for a second time in 1967 for her LP The Sweet Primeroses. This version was also included on her compilations A Favourite Garland and Fountain of Snow.

Joshua Burnell sang High Germany on 20 March 2017 in his “a folk song a week” song cycle. This was included in 2018 on his album Songs From the Seasons and in 2021 with re-recorded vocals on Seasons Vol. 2 Spring. He noted:

On one of the first occasions I met Martin, I proudly handed him a copy of Songs From the Seasons, beaming from ear-to-ear, delighted that such a legend of folk would hear my arrangements of these treasured songs, including … High Germany,the song, the cultural baton, handed from Martin to me so I could sprint on with it into the bright and ever ambitious future of English folk music!

More recently, I listened to the track and cringed to realise I had actually handed Martin Carthy a CD of me doing … a Martin Carthy impression. Ouch. On BBC Radio 2, Mark Radcliffe was very kind, and said I was, “;channelling Martin Carthy to great effect”. It was clearly a good impression.

For the re-release, I re-sung the vocal, hopefully doing an impression of myself but still channelling that raw magic that Martin put into these songs.
