Frank Purslow and John Pearse sang Young Ramble-Away in 1960 on their Folklore Records album Rap-a-Tap-Tap. Purslow noted:

The text has been collected from a broadside version: Birmingham Fair. The tune is surely one of the most attractive of English love songs.

Shirley Collins sang Rambleaway in 1963 on her Topic EP Heroes in Love. Like all tracks of this EP, it was included in her compilation Fountain of Snow and on the CD reissue of The Sweet Primeroses. Shirley and Dolly Collins recorded this song for a second time in 1969 for their album Anthems in Eden. This version was included in 1999 on the anthology Troubadours of British Folk Vol. 1.

Cyril Tawney sang Young Rambleaway in 1965 at a concert presented by the English Folk, Dance and Song Society at the Royal Festival Hall, London on 4 June 1965 which was released in the same year on the EMI/HMV album Folksound of Britain. He also sang it in 1976 on his Trailer album of seduction songs from the Baring-Gould manuscripts, Down Among the Barley Straw where he noted:.

The Young Tradition sang this song as Derry Down Fair on their eponymous debut album of 1966, The Young Tradition. They noted:
