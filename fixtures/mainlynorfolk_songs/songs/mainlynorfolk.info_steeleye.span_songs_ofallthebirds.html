Of All the Birds

[ Roud 496 ; Ballad Index ChWI141 ; Wiltshire 180 , 902 ; Folkinfo 369 ; Thomas Ravenscroft]

Sabine Baring-Gould, H. Fleetwood Sheppard: Songs of the West

Tim Hart and Maddy Prior sang Of All the Birds in 1971 on their third duo album, Summer Solstice. Maddy Prior returned to the song in 2012 when she, Hannah James and Giles Lewin sang it on their CD 3 for Joy.

Martyn Wyndham-Read, The Druids or Gerry Fox sang The Own in 1972 on their Argo album Songs and Music of the Redcoats.

Iona and Peter Opie comment in their Oxford Dictionary of Nursery Rhymes (Oxford University Press, 1951, 2nd ed., 1997, p. 97 (rhyme 50)) on the first verse:

The Millen Family sang The Old Owl on their 2001 album Down Yonder Green Lane. George Frampton noted:

rhis is more typical of the ‘glee’ singing which would have been part and parcel of the old Catch Club repertoire. The first verse was written by Thomas Ravenscoft in his Deuteromelia published in 1609 under the name Of All the Birds. The extra verses were conjured up from collective memory.

Coope, Simpson, Fraser & Freya sang The Owl, “from Thomas Ravencroftâs Deuteromelia 7 (1609)”, on their 2014 No Masters album Hark Hark.

Sound Tradition sang Of All the Birds in 2017 on their CD Well Met, My Friend. They noted:

Ravenscroft was a collector and writer of songs; this one appeared in his collection Deuteromelia, published in 1609. It seems also to have been popular as a nursery rhyme. It is unusual that the owl should receive such praise as it was considered a bird of ill omen in Britain at this period.
