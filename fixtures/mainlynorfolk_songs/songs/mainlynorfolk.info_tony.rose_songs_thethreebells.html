The Three Bells (Jimmy Brown)

[ Roud 19877 ; Jean Villard, Bert Reisfeld]

This hymn was first published in 1952 by Les Compagnons de la Chanson under the French title Let trois cloches. Jim Ed Brown and his sisters Maxine and Bonnie hat a #1 hit with it in 1959. Tony Rose sang it on his fourth album, Poor Fellows. He commented in the recordâs sleeve notes:

Another “classic” of its kind! Much recorded and parodied, but the original outshines all imitations. A great favourite of the late Arthur Parrot of Barnsley.

Lyrics

Tony Rose sings The Three Bells

There is a village hidden deep in the valley,
Among the pine trees half forlorn,
And there on a sunny morning
Little Jimmy Brown was born.
So his parents took him to the chapel
When he was only one day old,
And the priest blessed the little fellow:
“Welcome, Jimmy, to the fold.”

Chorus:
(dong, dong, dong, dong) All the chapel bells were ringing
In the little valley town,
And the song that they were singing
Was for baby Jimmy Brown.
And the little congregation,
Prayed for guidance from above:
“Lead us not into temptation,
Bless this hour of meditation,
Guide him with eternal love.”

There is a village hidden deep in the valley,
Beneath the mountains high above.
And there, twenty years thereafter,
Jimmy was to meet his love.
Many friends were gathered in the chapel
And many tears of joy were shed
In June, on a Sunday morning,
When Jimmy and his bride were wed.
