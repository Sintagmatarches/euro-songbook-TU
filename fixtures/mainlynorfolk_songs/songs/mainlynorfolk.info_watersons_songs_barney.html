Send Back My Barney to Me / My Johnny / My Bonnie Lies Over the Ocean

[ Roud 1422 ; Henry H7 ; Ballad Index HHH007 ; Folkinfo 442 ; DT MYBONNIE ; Mudcat 21476 ; Harry Clifton]

Send Back My Barney to Me / My Barney Lies Over the Ocean is a music hall song written and performed by Harry Clifton in c.1860.

It was later remade as the now more popular song My Bonnie Lies Over the Ocean, copyrighted in 1881 as Bring Back My Bonnie to Me by Charles E. Pratt under the dual pseudonyms H. J. Fuller and J. T. Wood. Associating the lyrics of this song with Charles Edward Stuart (‘Bonnie Prince Charlie’) seems to be a red herring.

The Leake County Revelers recorded My Bonnie Lies Over the Ocean on 25 October 1927 for their 10" shellac album Columbia 15227-D. This track was included in 2015 as the title track on the Nehi anthology of British songs in the USA, My Bonnie Lies Over the Ocean.

Cecilia Costello of Birmingham sang My Johnny an a BBC recording made my Marie Slocombe and Patrick Shuldham-Shaw on 30 November 1951. This recording was included in 1975 on her Leader album Cecilia Costello and, together with a second recording made by Peter Kennedy on 11 August 1951, in 2014 on her Musical Traditions anthology Old Fashioned Songs. Rod Stradling noted in the latterâs booklet:

Not a song much found in the oral tradition, with only 25 Roud entries—although my parentsâ generation knew it well as My Bonny Lies Over the Ocean, with its “Bring back, bring back” chorus. This certainly didnât have the fine aeolian tune Mrs Costello uses here, though her text has more than a hint of Victorian sentimentality. It was known in England and the USA, and Charles Demspey, from Derry, is mentioned as the only Irish singer. The only other recorded version is by Chris Bouchillon of Atlanta, Georgia (Old Hat CD 1005), who tags a parody verse and chorus onto the end of his talking blues, Born in Hard Luck.

Isla Cameron sang Bring Back My Johnny in 1962 on her Prestige album The Best of Isla Cameron. Ewan MacColl noted:

Writing in a recent issue of the Journal of the English Folk Dance and Song Society, a collector has suggested that this piece might well be the traditional basis of the American student song, My Bonnie Lies Over the Ocean. Since the song has a somewhat modern ring to it, it is equally possible that it is a derivative of the student song.

Hedy West sang Blow Ye Gently Winds in 1966 on her Topic album Pretty Saro and Other Appalachian Ballads. It was also included in 2011 on her Fellside anthology Ballads & Songs From the Appalachians. She noted on the original album:
