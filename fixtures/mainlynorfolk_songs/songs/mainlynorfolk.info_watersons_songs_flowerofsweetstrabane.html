The Flower of Sweet Strabane

[ Roud 2745 ; G/D 4:722 ; Henry H224a ; Ballad Index HHH224a ; DT FLWRSTRB ; Mudcat 8569 , 26566 ; trad.]

Gale Huntington, Lani Herrmann, John Moulden: Sam HenryГўВҖВҷs Songs of the People

John McGettigan sang Martha, the Flower of Sweet Strabane in a 1936 recording made in Philadelphia. It was included in 1979 on the Topic album of classic recordings of Irish traditional music in America, John McGettigan and His Irish Minstrels.

Margaret Barry sang The Flower of Sweet Strabane to Ewan MacColl on 10 March 1955. This recording was included a year later on her Riverside album Songs of an Irish Tinker Lady, and in 1965 on her Topic album with Michael Gordon Her Mantle So Green. An earlier recording made by Peter Kennedy in Dundalk, County Louth, Ireland, in 1952 was included in 1998 on her Rounder anthology I Sang Through the Fairs. The last albumГўВҖВҷs booklet noted:

The town of Strabane is in County Tyrone, Northern Ireland; near the border of County Donegal in the Irish Republic. This song has been circulating among Irish singers since the 1840s and was first published in a Derry newspaper in 1909.

Margaret loved to sing unaccompanied in the traditional Irish style. Sadly, few recordings were ever made of her singing without her banjo.

Paddy Tunney sang The Flower of Sweet Strabane in 1965 on his Topic album of Irish traditional ballads, A Wild BeeГўВҖВҷs Nest. Sean OГўВҖВҷBoyle noted:

There is one idea that underlies song after song in Ulster—the idea that the poets own lovely locality has produced the fairest woman in all Ireland. Sometimes she is The Rose of Moneymore, sometimes The Maid of Ballydoo or The Flower of Magherally. Paddy sings of lovely Martha whose coldness sends a lovelorn poet away from Sweet Strabane to exile in America. The tune is Re Mode, pentatonic.

Norma Waterson sang Flower of Sweet Strabane in 2000 on her third solo album, Bright Shiny Morning. This track was also included on the BBC Radio 2 Folk Awards 2001 compilation. Norma Waterson noted on her album:

Kerry Smith sang The Flower of Sweet Strabane on her 2020 Folk E.P..

Polly Paulusma sang The Flower of Sweet Strabane on her 2021 album of folk songs that influenced Angela Carter, Invisible Music.

The Weaving sang The Flower of Sweet Strabane on their 2026 album DlГғВәth agus Inneach (Warp & Weft). CГғВЎit NГғВӯ Riain noted:

I first heard my father singing this song in the small hours of the morning after a night of music by our fire at home. There was a beauty that was coming from both the song and the way he sang it that enchanted me from the first line. I started singing it after that night, my felt memory of that moment as my inner guide into the song. I only then heard the many other versions that have been recorded that were a lot more upbeat and metered. I feel that the emotional depth of the song can be transmitted more powerfully with space, allowing the pulse to speak beyond the beat.
