Let the Bulgine Run

[ Roud 810 ; Ballad Index LoF029 ; Folkinfo 898 ; DT BULGNE , BULGNE2 ; Mudcat 16554 ; trad.]

The Watersons sang the hauling shanty Let the Bulgine Run in 1964 in an early live performance. This recording was included in 2004 on their 4 CD anthology Mighty River of Song. According to Stan Hugillâs Shanties From the Seven Seas, ‘bulgine’ is American slang for a railway engine.

Ray and Archie Fisher sang Let the Bulgine Run at a folk concert at Edinburgh Usher Hall that was released in 1964 too on the Waverley LP Folk Festival.

Jolly Jack sang Clear the Track and Let the Bulgine Run in 1983 on their Fellside album Rolling Down to Old Maui.

Dave Webber sang The Bulgine Shanty in 1993 on his and Anni Fentimanâs album Together Solo. They noted:

The shanty ranks among Daveâs favourites and is well documented in Stan Hugillâs Shanties From the Seven Seas. The word Bulgine is said to originate from the Negro word for engine or bull engine.

Jim Mageean and Johnny Collins sang Clear the Track at the International Shanty Festival Workum in 1992, 1993 or 1995. This recording was included on their 1996 album celebrating 21 years together, Coming of Age. And Johnny Collins with Dave Webber and Pete Watkinson sang Eliza Lee in 1996 on their album Shanties & Songs of the Sea.

Bellowhead sang this shanty as Let Her Run in 2014 on their CD Revival. They noted:

Adapted from the hauling shanty Let the Bulgine Run, ‘bulgine’ being slang for ‘engine’. There are many variants of this song under many names such as Eliza Lee or Clear the Track including, bizarrely, a music hall version with Biblical verses.

Tickled Pink sang Let the Bullgine Runx and Johnny Come Down to Hilo in 2005 on their Talking Elephant album Terpsichore Polyhymnia. A live recording from the Mill Banbury in April 2002 was included in the same year on Simon Careâs anthology The “Box” Set. They noted:

Because of our concern about global warming, the good people of Northampton have been preparing for the time when the sea will swallow the Fens and Corby-by-Sea is a reality. As such we have learnt these two sea shanties to complement the lighthouse already standing in Northampton.

Doggerland sang Clear the Track on their 2017 CD No Sadness of Farewell. They noted:

This is one of many shanties that probably started life as a railroad song (bulgine being an American slang term for a railway engine).

The Salts sang Bulgine Run on their 2018 CD Brave. A live recording from Wiltonâs Music Hall, London, on 13 April 2023, was included in the following year on their album Live in London Town.
