Margaret Jeffrey sang When I Was a Young Maid to Peter Kennedy and Hamish Henderson in the berryfields at Blairgowrie, Perthshire, in July 1955. This recording was included in 2012 on the Topic anthology Good People, Take Warning (The Voice of the People Volume 23). Steve Roud noted:

The Female Drummer was collected regularly in England and Scotland, but more rarely in Ireland or North America, and appeared on numerous broadsides and garlands, dating back to about 1800. The broadsides claim that she fought with the Duke of York at the Siege of Valenciennes, which took place in 1793 during the French Revolution, but the traditional texts have all discarded this historical detail. The motif of women dressing as men is fairly common in British folk song, but the reason is usually to accompany or find a sweetheart who has gone to war. Our drummer girl, however, seems to have enlisted for the sheer hell of it.

Harry Cox sang The Female Drummer at home in Catfield, Norfolk, in 1955 to Ewan MacColl. This recording was included in 2000 on his Topic anthology, The Bonny Labouring Boy. Steve Roud noted:

The earliest known printing is on an Evans (London) broadside of about 1810 in the Madden Collection at Cambridge University Library.
