When First I Came to Caledonia

[ Roud - ; DT FRSTCALE ; Mudcat 182 ; trad. Cape Breton]

Songs & Stories from Deep Cove, Cape Breton

This song is not about Caledonia as Scotland. It is a song from Cape Breton Island, Nova Scotia, and refers to the Caledonia coal mines in Glace Bay with #3 being one of the pits. The “cask of brandy” verse seems to have been borrowed from Peggy Gordon, which is another Nova Scotian song. There are some other generic floating verses.

Tony Cuffe sang When First I Went to Caledonia in 1988 as the title track of his solo album When First I Went to Caledonia. He noted:

A song from Cape Breton Island, Nova Scotia with a rather misleading title. ‘Calwdonia’ in this case refers not to Scotland, but to the Caledonia Coal Mines in Glace Bay, and ‘Number Three’ is one of the pits.

Scataree is a small island just off the coast of Cape Breton, now uninhabited except for a lighthouse keeper, and Boulardrie is one of the more fertile farming areas.

From the book Songs & Stories from Deep Cove, Cape Breton by Amby Thomas.
